How to Rent Bicycles or Electric Scooters for Sightseeing in Guangzhou

  • Home
  • Travel Blog

Guangzhou, one of China's most vibrant cities, is an exciting destination for travelers looking to explore its blend of modernity and tradition. Whether you're visiting iconic landmarks, strolling along the Pearl River, or navigating the city's lively streets, renting a bicycle or electric scooter can be a great way to see the sights at your own pace. With many bike-sharing platforms and convenient rental locations, the city makes it easy to explore on two wheels. Here’s a comprehensive guide on how to rent a bicycle or electric scooter in Guangzhou, making your trip more fun, efficient, and eco-friendly.

Understanding the Bike-Sharing Culture in Guangzhou

Guangzhou, like many other Chinese cities, has adopted the bike-sharing model, which has become increasingly popular in recent years. Several companies offer bicycle and electric scooter rentals via mobile apps, which makes the entire process seamless and accessible to both locals and tourists. These bike-sharing services have contributed significantly to the city's efforts to promote eco-friendly transportation and reduce traffic congestion.

Popular Bike-Sharing Platforms

Some of the most popular bike-sharing companies in Guangzhou are:

  1. Ofo (Ofo): A well-known bike-sharing brand that offers both traditional bicycles and electric bikes. Their service is reliable, and you can find bikes scattered around the city. The Ofo app is available in English, making it convenient for tourists.
  2. Mobike (Mobike): Mobike is another popular bike-sharing company, offering a wide variety of bikes, including traditional pedal bikes and electric bicycles. The bikes are equipped with GPS, and their availability is spread across major tourist spots.
  3. Hello Bike (Hello Chuxing): Hello Bike offers both bicycles and e-scooters, and is considered one of the best platforms for tourists. It’s user-friendly and operates in many tourist-friendly areas across Guangzhou.
  4. DiDi Bike (DiDi Cycle): Known for its ride-hailing services, DiDi also offers bike rentals and electric scooters in select areas. Their app supports multiple languages, including English.

Types of Bikes and Scooters Available

When it comes to renting a bike or scooter in Guangzhou, there are typically two main options: traditional bicycles and electric bikes or scooters.

  • Traditional Bicycles: If you enjoy a more active experience and prefer pedaling, traditional bicycles are available for rent across various platforms. These bikes are lightweight and perfect for short rides around the city.
  • Electric Bicycles: Electric bikes offer a convenient and faster way to navigate Guangzhou, especially if you’re covering longer distances or want to reduce the effort required for pedaling. They are easy to use and usually have a battery that lasts for several hours, depending on usage.
  • Electric Scooters: Electric scooters are becoming increasingly popular for tourists who want to experience the city in a fun and efficient way. They are ideal for short-distance travel and navigating busy areas with ease.

How to Rent a Bicycle or Electric Scooter in Guangzhou

Renting a bike or electric scooter in Guangzhou is relatively simple, thanks to the widespread availability of rental services and easy-to-use mobile apps. Below are the steps involved in the rental process:

Step 1: Download the App

The first step in renting a bike or electric scooter in Guangzhou is downloading the relevant app for the bike-sharing platform. Most platforms have their apps available for download on both Android and iOS devices. Here’s a brief rundown of the apps for the mentioned services:

  • Ofo: Download the "Ofo" app from your device’s app store.
  • Mobike: The "Mobike" app is available on the App Store or Google Play Store.
  • Hello Bike: Simply search for "Hello Bike" in your app store.
  • DiDi Bike: The "DiDi" app, available on most app stores, also supports bike rentals in Guangzhou.

Once downloaded, create an account by providing some basic information, such as your phone number. Some apps may ask for ID verification, which can typically be done using your passport or an ID card.

Step 2: Locate a Bike or Scooter

Once the app is set up, you can use it to locate nearby bikes or electric scooters available for rent. The app will show you a map of your location and highlight the available bikes or scooters in the vicinity. Many bikes are stationed at popular tourist spots, parks, and transport hubs, such as Guangzhou East Railway Station or the Canton Tower.

Step 3: Unlock and Start the Ride

Once you've located a bike or scooter near you, it’s time to unlock it. Simply scan the QR code located on the bike’s frame or scooter using the app, and it will automatically unlock. For electric bikes and scooters, the app will also display the battery levels, so you can decide whether to go for a regular bike or an electric one based on the distance you plan to travel.

The app will also inform you of the rental rate, which may vary depending on the platform and the type of bike or scooter you choose.

Step 4: Ride and Explore the City

Once you’ve unlocked your bike or scooter, it’s time to start exploring Guangzhou. You can take your time enjoying the city’s scenic routes, visiting famous landmarks, or simply cruising along the Pearl River. If you are renting an electric bike or scooter, you’ll notice the smooth, effortless experience, making your ride much faster and easier.

Step 5: Parking and Ending the Ride

Once you’re done with your ride, park the bike or scooter in a designated area, ensuring it is not obstructing pedestrians or vehicles. Most bike-sharing services encourage users to park bikes in specified bike racks or hubs to avoid them being left in inconvenient spots. After parking, open the app again to end your ride. The app will automatically calculate the fare and deduct the amount from your linked payment method.

If you’re renting an electric bike or scooter, make sure it’s fully parked and turned off before you end the ride on the app.

Renting Bicycles or Electric Scooters in Guangzhou: Tips and Recommendations

While renting a bike or electric scooter in Guangzhou is generally an easy and enjoyable process, there are a few things to keep in mind for a safe and smooth experience:

  1. Language Barriers: Many bike-sharing apps have English language options, but some signs or instructions in public spaces may be in Chinese. If you're not comfortable reading Chinese, make sure your phone’s translation app is set up, or ask locals for help.
  2. Weather Considerations: Guangzhou can get quite hot and humid, especially during the summer months. If you're planning to cycle or ride an electric scooter, it’s best to do so during the cooler parts of the day, such as early mornings or late evenings.
  3. Helmets: While bike-sharing services generally do not provide helmets, it’s advisable to wear one for safety. You can purchase or rent helmets from local shops or bring your own.
  4. Traffic Awareness: Guangzhou’s streets can be busy, especially during peak hours. Always follow local traffic rules and stay aware of your surroundings. Use bike lanes whenever possible and be cautious around traffic.
  5. Battery Check for Electric Scooters: If you're renting an electric scooter or bike, always check the battery level before starting your ride. Choose one with sufficient charge, especially if you're planning a long journey.

Where to Ride a Bicycle or Electric Scooter in Guangzhou

Guangzhou is a great city for cycling or riding an electric scooter, with many scenic routes and bike-friendly locations. Here are some of the best areas to explore:

  • Canton Tower: Ride along the river and enjoy stunning views of the iconic Canton Tower.
  • Pearl River: Cycling along the Pearl River is a wonderful way to see the city, with many parks and riverside pathways to explore.
  • Yuexiu Park: This large urban park offers both paved paths and nature trails, perfect for a relaxed ride.
  • Shamian Island: Known for its colonial architecture and peaceful atmosphere, it’s a charming place for a casual bike ride.

By renting a bicycle or electric scooter, you’ll have the freedom to explore Guangzhou at your own pace and experience its sights in a more intimate way.
