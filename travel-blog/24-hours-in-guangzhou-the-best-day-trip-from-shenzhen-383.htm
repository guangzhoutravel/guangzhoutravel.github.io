24 Hours in Guangzhou: The Best Day Trip from Shenzhen

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Guangzhou, the sprawling capital of Guangdong Province, is a city where ancient traditions collide with futuristic skyscrapers. Just an hour away by high-speed train from Shenzhen, it’s the perfect destination for a whirlwind day trip. Whether you’re a foodie, history buff, or shopaholic, Guangzhou delivers an unforgettable 24-hour adventure. Here’s how to make the most of your day.

Morning: Dive into Cantonese Culture

Start with Dim Sum at a Legendary Teahouse

No visit to Guangzhou is complete without indulging in dim sum. Head to Lian Xiang Lou (莲香楼) or Tao Tao Ju (陶陶居), two of the city’s most iconic teahouses. Order classics like har gow (shrimp dumplings), siu mai (pork and shrimp dumplings), and char siu bao (barbecue pork buns). Pair your meal with a pot of fragrant jasmine tea for the ultimate Cantonese breakfast experience.

Explore Shamian Island

After breakfast, take a short taxi ride to Shamian Island, a tranquil oasis with colonial-era architecture. Once a concession area for European powers, the tree-lined streets and pastel-colored buildings make it a photographer’s paradise. Don’t miss the iconic Our Lady of Lourdes Chapel and the bronze statues depicting life in old Guangzhou.

Visit the Temple of the Six Banyan Trees

Next, head to the Temple of the Six Banyan Trees, a 1,400-year-old Buddhist temple. The highlight is the Flower Pagoda, a towering structure adorned with colorful eaves. Climb to the top for panoramic views of the city, or simply soak in the serene atmosphere of the temple grounds.

Afternoon: Modern Marvels and Shopping Sprees

Lunch at a Cantonese Banquet Restaurant

For lunch, treat yourself to a Cantonese banquet at Panxi Restaurant (泮溪酒家), famous for its lakeside setting and exquisite dishes. Try the roast goose, steamed fish, and stir-fried milk—a Guangzhou specialty.

Ascend the Canton Tower

No trip to Guangzhou is complete without visiting the Canton Tower, one of the tallest structures in the world. Take the elevator to the observation deck for breathtaking views of the Pearl River and the city skyline. If you’re feeling adventurous, try the Sky Drop, a thrilling free-fall ride on the tower’s exterior.

Shop Till You Drop on Beijing Road

Afterward, dive into Guangzhou’s shopping scene at Beijing Road, a bustling pedestrian street lined with malls, boutiques, and street vendors. Look for traditional handicrafts, silk products, and local snacks like egg tarts and rice noodle rolls.

Evening: Riverside Romance and Night Markets

Stroll Along the Pearl River

As the sun sets, take a leisurely walk along the Pearl River. The illuminated bridges and skyscrapers create a magical atmosphere. For a more immersive experience, hop on a Pearl River night cruise and enjoy the city lights from the water.

Feast at Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street

End your day with a food crawl at Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street, a culinary hotspot. Sample street food like clay pot rice, fish balls, and mango pomelo sago dessert. Don’t forget to try double-skin milk, a creamy Cantonese dessert.

Unwind at a Rooftop Bar

If you still have energy, head to a rooftop bar like Tian Bar at the Rosewood Guangzhou for cocktails with a view. Sip on a lychee martini as you reflect on your whirlwind day in this dynamic city.

Guangzhou may be massive, but with this itinerary, you’ll experience its best highlights in just 24 hours. From ancient temples to futuristic towers, every moment promises adventure.
