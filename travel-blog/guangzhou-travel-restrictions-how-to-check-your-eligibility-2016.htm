Guangzhou Travel Restrictions: How to Check Your Eligibility

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Guangzhou, a bustling metropolis in southern China, is a top destination for travelers seeking a mix of modern skyscrapers, historic landmarks, and vibrant Cantonese culture. However, like many major cities, Guangzhou has implemented travel restrictions to ensure public health and safety. Whether you're planning a business trip, a family vacation, or a solo adventure, understanding these restrictions is crucial. Here’s everything you need to know about Guangzhou’s travel policies and how to check if you’re eligible to visit.

Understanding Guangzhou’s Travel Restrictions

Guangzhou’s travel restrictions are primarily influenced by national and regional policies, including health protocols, visa requirements, and pandemic-related measures. These rules can change frequently, so staying updated is essential.

Current Entry Requirements

As of now, travelers entering Guangzhou must comply with the following:

  1. Valid Visa or Entry Permit – Most foreign nationals require a visa to enter China, though some may qualify for visa-free transit. Check with the Chinese embassy or consulate in your country for the latest updates.
  2. Health Declaration Form – All travelers must complete a health declaration form, usually via the China Customs app or upon arrival.
  3. COVID-19 Testing – Some travelers may need to present a negative PCR test taken within a specified timeframe before departure.
  4. Quarantine Rules – Depending on your travel history and current health status, you may be subject to quarantine upon arrival.

How to Check Your Eligibility

Before booking your trip, verify your eligibility by following these steps:

Step 1: Review China’s Visa Policy

Visit the official website of the Chinese embassy in your country to confirm whether you need a visa and if any special entry permits apply.

Step 2: Monitor Travel Advisories

Check platforms like the U.S. State Department’s Travel Advisory or the UK Foreign Office’s travel advice for updates on Guangzhou’s entry rules.

Step 3: Use Guangzhou’s Official Health Apps

Download apps like "Suikang" (穗康) or "Yuekang Code" (粤康码) to register your health status before arrival. These apps often provide real-time updates on travel restrictions.

Step 4: Contact Your Airline

Airlines operating flights to Guangzhou (such as China Southern Airlines) usually have updated information on entry requirements. Confirm with them before departure.

Popular Attractions and Their Entry Policies

Guangzhou is home to iconic sites like the Canton Tower, Shamian Island, and Yuexiu Park. However, some attractions may have additional entry rules.

Canton Tower

One of the tallest structures in the world, the Canton Tower offers breathtaking city views. Currently, visitors must:
- Present a green health code (obtained via Suikang or Yuekang Code).
- Wear masks in indoor areas.
- Book tickets online in advance to avoid overcrowding.

Shamian Island

This historic colonial district is a must-visit for architecture lovers. Entry requirements include:
- No mandatory health checks, but social distancing is encouraged.
- Some cafes and shops may require temperature checks.

Baiyun Mountain

A favorite among nature enthusiasts, Baiyun Mountain has relaxed restrictions but recommends:
- Avoiding peak hours.
- Bringing your own water and snacks, as some vendors may have limited operations.

Tips for a Smooth Travel Experience

  1. Stay Updated – Rules can change overnight, so subscribe to Guangzhou’s tourism bureau newsletters or follow local news outlets.
  2. Pack Essentials – Carry extra masks, hand sanitizer, and printed copies of your health documents.
  3. Plan for Delays – Health screenings at airports and train stations may take longer than usual.

By following these guidelines, you can ensure a hassle-free visit to Guangzhou and enjoy all that this dynamic city has to offer. Safe travels!
