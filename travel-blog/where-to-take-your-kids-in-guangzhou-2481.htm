Where to Take Your Kids in Guangzhou

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Guangzhou, the vibrant capital of Guangdong Province, is a fantastic destination for families. With its mix of modern attractions, cultural landmarks, and lush green spaces, there’s no shortage of fun and educational experiences for kids. Whether you're a local or a visitor, here’s a curated list of the best places to take your little ones in this dynamic city.

Top Family-Friendly Attractions

1. Chimelong Safari Park

One of the largest wildlife theme parks in the world, Chimelong Safari Park is a must-visit for animal-loving kids. The park features over 20,000 animals, including rare species like white tigers and giant pandas.

Highlights:

  • Safari on Wheels: Take a guided tram tour through open habitats where lions, giraffes, and elephants roam freely.
  • Panda Village: Meet adorable giant pandas in a carefully designed habitat.
  • Animal Performances: Enjoy entertaining shows featuring sea lions, parrots, and more.

2. Guangzhou Zoo

For a more budget-friendly wildlife experience, Guangzhou Zoo offers a great alternative. While smaller than Chimelong, it still houses a diverse range of animals and a fun amusement section.

Kid-Friendly Features:

  • Children’s Play Area: Mini rides and interactive exhibits.
  • Aquarium Section: Colorful fish and marine life displays.

3. Canton Tower

A visit to Guangzhou isn’t complete without seeing the iconic Canton Tower. While it might seem like an adult attraction, kids will love the thrilling views and fun activities.

What to Do:

  • Sky Drop: A controlled free-fall ride that’s exhilarating for older kids.
  • Bubble Tram: A slow-moving glass pod ride around the tower’s exterior.
  • Observation Deck: Stunning panoramic views of the city.

Educational & Cultural Spots

4. Guangdong Science Center

Ranked as one of the largest science museums globally, this interactive hub makes learning fun.

Exhibits Kids Love:

  • Children’s World: Hands-on experiments and play zones.
  • Space Exploration: Simulated rocket launches and planetarium shows.

5. Chen Clan Ancestral Hall (Guangdong Folk Art Museum)

A beautiful introduction to traditional Lingnan architecture and crafts.

Engaging Activities:

  • Wood Carving & Embroidery Displays: Visually stunning for all ages.
  • Workshops: Occasionally hosts kid-friendly craft sessions.

6. Yuexiu Park

A sprawling urban park perfect for picnics, boat rides, and light hiking.

Family Highlights:

  • Five Rams Statue: The symbol of Guangzhou—great for photos.
  • Zhenhai Tower: A historic site with city views.
  • Paddle Boats: Rent a boat for a relaxing lake trip.

Fun & Adventure

7. Happy Valley Guangzhou

This amusement park offers thrilling rides and themed zones for all ages.

Best Rides for Kids:

  • Magic Carpet: A gentle roller coaster for younger visitors.
  • Water Play Area: Splash zones for hot summer days.

8. Baomo Garden

A serene escape with traditional gardens, koi ponds, and intricate sculptures.

Why Kids Enjoy It:

  • Feeding the Fish: Buy fish food and watch koi swarm.
  • Mini Waterfalls & Bridges: Perfect for exploration.

9. Pearl River Night Cruise

A relaxing way to see Guangzhou’s skyline lit up at night.

Kid-Friendly Tips:

  • Choose shorter cruises (1-hour options).
  • Bring snacks and enjoy the breeze on the open deck.

Indoor Play Zones

10. Baby Boss City

An interactive role-play center where kids can try different professions.

Popular Roles:

  • Firefighter, chef, doctor—complete with costumes and mini workplaces.

11. Kidzooona

A soft-play paradise for toddlers and younger children.

Features:

  • Ball pits, slides, and climbing structures.
  • Supervised play areas for peace of mind.

Seasonal & Special Events

12. Guangzhou International Light Festival

If visiting in winter, don’t miss this dazzling display of illuminated art.

Tips:

  • Bundle up and bring a stroller for tired little legs.
  • Weekdays are less crowded.

13. Flower Markets During Chinese New Year

A vibrant experience with festive decorations and treats.

Kid-Friendly Aspects:

  • Candy stalls and toy vendors.
  • Lion dance performances.

Food Stops for Picky Eaters

14. Dim Sum at Taotaoju

Introduce kids to bite-sized favorites like shrimp dumplings (har gow) and BBQ pork buns (char siu bao).

15. Dessert at Sweet Dynasty

Try mango pomelo sago or egg tarts—sweet wins for kids.

16. Western Options at The Happy Monk

Burgers, pasta, and pancakes for less adventurous eaters.

Practical Tips for Visiting Guangzhou with Kids

  • Stroller Accessibility: Many attractions are stroller-friendly, but some older sites have stairs.
  • Public Transport: Metro is efficient; taxis are affordable for shorter distances.
  • Weather Prep: Summers are hot—bring hats, sunscreen, and water.
  • Language: Learn basic Mandarin phrases or use translation apps.

With so much to see and do, Guangzhou is a playground for curious young minds. Whether it’s wildlife adventures, cultural discoveries, or just running around in a park, your kids will leave with unforgettable memories.
