Guangzhou’s Spring Festival Travel Guide

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Spring Festival, or Chun Jie, is the most vibrant time of the year in Guangzhou. As the capital of Guangdong Province, this megacity blends ancient traditions with modern flair, offering travelers an unforgettable experience. From dazzling flower markets to mouthwatering Cantonese feasts, here’s your ultimate guide to celebrating the Lunar New Year in Guangzhou.

Why Visit Guangzhou During Spring Festival?

Guangzhou transforms into a festive wonderland during Chun Jie. The city’s subtropical climate makes it a comfortable winter escape, while its deep-rooted Cantonese culture ensures a unique celebration. Whether you’re chasing lion dances, indulging in dim sum, or hunting for red envelopes (hongbao), Guangzhou delivers an authentic Spring Festival vibe.

Top Spring Festival Experiences in Guangzhou

1. Flower Markets (Hua Shi)

No Spring Festival in Guangzhou is complete without visiting the iconic flower markets. These bustling bazaars pop up across the city, with the most famous ones located along Beijing Road and Tianhe District. Locals believe buying flowers—especially peach blossoms, kumquats, and orchids—brings prosperity. The markets also sell festive decorations, making them perfect for souvenir hunting.

Pro Tip: Visit on New Year’s Eve for the liveliest atmosphere, but expect crowds!

2. Temple of the Six Banyan Trees (Liu Rong Si)

For a spiritual start to the year, head to this 1,500-year-old Buddhist temple. During Spring Festival, devotees flock here to pray for good fortune. The temple’s Flower Pagoda is a stunning backdrop for photos, and the incense-filled air adds to the mystical ambiance.

3. Canton Tower Light Show

Guangzhou’s skyline dazzles during Chun Jie, with the Canton Tower putting on a spectacular light show. The tower’s LED display syncs with fireworks (where permitted), creating a mesmerizing spectacle. For the best views, book a ticket to the observation deck or dine at one of the tower’s revolving restaurants.

4. Dim Sum Feasts

Spring Festival is all about family reunions, and nothing says “celebration” like a Cantonese dim sum banquet. Head to Lian Xiang Lou or Tao Tao Ju for classics like har gow (shrimp dumplings) and char siu bao (barbecue pork buns). Many restaurants offer special Lunar New Year menus, so reservations are a must.

Off-the-Beaten-Path Gems

1. Xiguan Antique Street

Escape the tourist crowds and explore Xiguan, Guangzhou’s historic quarter. The area’s Qing Dynasty-era mansions and cobblestone streets offer a glimpse into old Guangzhou. During Spring Festival, local artisans sell handmade crafts, from paper cuttings to calligraphy scrolls.

2. Baiyun Mountain Hike

Start the new year on an active note with a hike up Baiyun Mountain. The trails are decorated with red lanterns, and the summit offers panoramic views of the city. If you’re lucky, you might catch a traditional lion dance performance at the base of the mountain.

Practical Tips for Travelers

1. Transportation

Guangzhou’s metro system is efficient, but during Spring Festival, stations near popular attractions get packed. Consider using Didi (China’s Uber) or renting a bike via Mobike.

2. Weather Prep

Daytime temperatures hover around 15–20°C (59–68°F), but evenings can be chilly. Pack layers and a light jacket.

3. Cash vs. Digital Payments

While Alipay and WeChat Pay dominate, some small vendors only accept cash—especially in flower markets. Carry small bills for hongbao!

Final Thoughts

Guangzhou’s Spring Festival is a sensory overload in the best way possible. Whether you’re savoring pineapple buns at a teahouse or watching fireworks over the Pearl River, this city knows how to ring in the Lunar New Year with style. So grab your camera, wear something red, and dive into the festivities!
