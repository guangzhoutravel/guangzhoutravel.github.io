Top 10 Must-Do Activities in Guangzhou During Chinese New Year

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Guangzhou, the vibrant capital of Guangdong Province, transforms into a dazzling spectacle during Chinese New Year. From dazzling lantern displays to mouthwatering Cantonese feasts, the city offers unforgettable experiences for travelers. Whether you're a first-time visitor or a seasoned explorer, here are the top 10 must-do activities to make your Lunar New Year in Guangzhou truly special.

1. Stroll Through the Flower Markets at Yuexiu District

The Heart of Lunar New Year Traditions

No visit to Guangzhou during Chinese New Year is complete without exploring the iconic flower markets. These bustling bazaars, especially in Yuexiu District, are a feast for the senses. Locals believe buying flowers and plants—like peach blossoms, kumquat trees, and orchids—brings prosperity and good luck for the coming year.

What to Expect

  • Vibrant stalls selling festive decorations
  • Live performances and traditional lion dances
  • Aromatic street snacks like turnip cakes and sweet rice balls

2. Watch the Dazzling Lantern Festival at Liwan Lake Park

A Night of Enchantment

The Lantern Festival marks the grand finale of Chinese New Year celebrations. Head to Liwan Lake Park, where thousands of handcrafted lanterns illuminate the night sky. The displays often feature zodiac-themed designs, mythical creatures, and even interactive light installations.

Pro Tips

  • Arrive early to avoid crowds
  • Bring a camera for stunning night photography
  • Try yuanxiao (sweet glutinous rice dumplings) from nearby vendors

3. Feast on Cantonese New Year Delicacies

A Culinary Journey

Guangzhou is a foodie’s paradise, and Chinese New Year amplifies its culinary magic. Don’t miss these must-try dishes:

Must-Eat Dishes

  • Poon choi (a lavish layered pot with seafood, meats, and veggies)
  • Nian gao (sticky rice cake symbolizing growth and progress)
  • Jau gok (crispy fried dumplings for wealth)

Best Places to Dine

  • Lianxiang Lou for dim sum
  • Panxi Restaurant for traditional banquets

4. Experience the Chimelong Paradise New Year Parade

Family-Friendly Extravaganza

Chimelong Paradise, one of China’s top theme parks, hosts a spectacular New Year parade featuring giant floats, acrobats, and costumed performers. Kids (and adults!) will love the festive atmosphere and thrilling rides.

Highlights

  • Fireworks at midnight
  • Meet-and-greets with mascots like the Lucky Dragon

5. Visit the Temple of the Six Banyan Trees for Blessings

Spiritual Serenity

This ancient Buddhist temple becomes a hub of activity during Chinese New Year. Devotees light incense, pray for good fortune, and ring the giant bell for luck. The tranquil gardens offer a peaceful escape from the city’s hustle.

Rituals to Try

  • Tossing coins into the wishing well
  • Buying a red luck charm from the temple shop

6. Shop for Festive Souvenirs at Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street

Retail Therapy with a Cultural Twist

Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street, lined with historic Qilou buildings, is the perfect place to snag unique souvenirs. Look for red envelopes, paper cuttings, and embroidered silk items.

Bargaining Tips

  • Smile and be polite
  • Start at 50% of the asking price

7. Cruise the Pearl River for a Night of Lights

Skyline Views Like No Other

A nighttime Pearl River cruise offers breathtaking views of Guangzhou’s skyline adorned with festive lights. Many cruises serve special New Year dinners with live music.

Recommended Cruise Lines

  • Canton Pearl for luxury dining
  • Guangzhou Water Bus for budget-friendly options

8. Join the Baiyun Mountain Sunrise Hike

A Fresh Start to the Year

Locals hike Baiyun Mountain at dawn on New Year’s Day to welcome the first sunrise—a symbol of new beginnings. The panoramic city views are worth the early wake-up call.

Trail Tips

  • Wear comfortable shoes
  • Pack a thermos of hot tea

9. Marvel at the Canton Tower Light Show

A Modern Twist on Tradition

The Canton Tower, Guangzhou’s iconic landmark, puts on a high-tech light show synchronized to traditional music. It’s a mesmerizing blend of old and new.

Best Viewing Spots

  • Haixinsha Island
  • The tower’s observation deck

10. Learn Lion Dancing at a Local Workshop

Hands-On Cultural Immersion

Lion dancing is a cornerstone of Chinese New Year. Several cultural centers, like the Guangdong Folk Art Museum, offer short workshops where you can learn basic moves and even dress in a lion costume.

What You’ll Gain

  • A deeper appreciation for the art form
  • Epic photos for your social media
