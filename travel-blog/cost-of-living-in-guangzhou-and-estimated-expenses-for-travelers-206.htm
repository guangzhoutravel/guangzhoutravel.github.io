Cost of Living in Guangzhou and Estimated Expenses for Travelers

  • Home
  • Travel Blog

Guangzhou, one of China's largest and most economically developed cities, is a vibrant metropolis known for its rich history, modern skyline, and world-famous Cantonese cuisine. As a major commercial hub, the city attracts business professionals, expatriates, and tourists from all over the world. But how expensive is daily life in Guangzhou? And what can travelers expect to spend during their visit?

This guide breaks down the cost of living in Guangzhou and provides a detailed estimate of travel expenses for visitors, covering accommodation, food, transportation, attractions, and other essential costs.

Daily Cost of Living in Guangzhou

For those considering living in Guangzhou, the city offers a range of affordability depending on lifestyle choices. Whether you’re a budget-conscious individual, a mid-range expat, or someone looking for luxury, the city has options to suit different spending levels.

1. Housing Costs

The cost of housing varies widely depending on location, size, and building type. Areas in the city center, such as Tianhe, Zhujiang New Town, and Yuexiu, are significantly more expensive compared to suburban districts.

  • Rent for a one-bedroom apartment in the city center: ¥5,000 – ¥8,000 per month
  • Rent for a one-bedroom apartment in suburban areas: ¥2,500 – ¥4,500 per month
  • Shared accommodation (common among expats and students): ¥1,500 – ¥3,000 per month

Those who opt for high-end serviced apartments in premium locations like Zhujiang New Town should expect to pay ¥10,000 or more per month.

2. Food and Dining Expenses

Guangzhou is famous for its affordable yet delicious Cantonese cuisine, with options ranging from street food stalls to Michelin-starred restaurants.

  • Local breakfast (dim sum, baozi, or noodles): ¥10 – ¥30
  • Lunch at a casual restaurant: ¥30 – ¥80
  • Dinner at a mid-range restaurant: ¥100 – ¥300
  • Street food/snacks: ¥5 – ¥20 per item
  • Fine dining or luxury restaurant experience: ¥500+ per person

If you prefer cooking at home, groceries from local markets are relatively cheap:

  • 1kg of rice: ¥8 – ¥15
  • 1kg of chicken breast: ¥20 – ¥35
  • 1kg of apples: ¥10 – ¥20
  • Vegetables (per kg, varies by type): ¥5 – ¥15

3. Transportation Costs

Guangzhou has an efficient and affordable public transportation system, including metro, buses, and taxis.

  • Metro fare (per trip): ¥2 – ¥8 (based on distance)
  • Monthly metro pass: ¥180 – ¥250
  • Bus fare: ¥2 – ¥4 per ride
  • Taxi starting fare: ¥12, then ¥2.6 per km
  • Didi (ride-hailing app) for short trips: ¥20 – ¥50
  • Bicycle rental (per hour): ¥1 – ¥5

4. Utilities and Internet

  • Electricity, water, and gas (monthly for a small apartment): ¥300 – ¥600
  • High-speed internet (per month): ¥100 – ¥200
  • Mobile phone plan (data & calls): ¥50 – ¥200 per month

5. Entertainment and Leisure

  • Movie ticket: ¥40 – ¥80
  • Gym membership: ¥200 – ¥800 per month
  • KTV (karaoke) per hour: ¥100 – ¥400 (varies by location)
  • Bars and nightlife (drinks per glass): ¥50 – ¥100
  • Theme parks (Chimelong, Canton Tower experience, etc.): ¥150 – ¥500

Estimated Travel Costs for Tourists in Guangzhou

If you’re planning a trip to Guangzhou, your budget will depend on whether you’re traveling on a budget, mid-range, or luxury. Below is a breakdown of expected costs for different traveler types.

1. Accommodation Costs for Tourists

  • Budget hotels/hostels: ¥100 – ¥300 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: ¥400 – ¥800 per night
  • Luxury hotels (Five-star, high-end stays): ¥1,200 – ¥3,000+ per night

For budget travelers, areas like Haizhu, Baiyun, and parts of Liwan offer affordable hotels and hostels, while mid-range travelers may prefer Tianhe and Yuexiu for more comfort and convenience.

2. Food and Drinks for Tourists

A typical food budget per day could look like this:

  • Budget travelers (street food and local eateries): ¥50 – ¥150 per day
  • Mid-range travelers (casual restaurants, occasional fine dining): ¥200 – ¥500 per day
  • Luxury travelers (high-end restaurants, rooftop bars): ¥800+ per day

3. Sightseeing and Attractions Costs

Some of Guangzhou’s best attractions and experiences come with various price ranges:

  • Canton Tower observation deck: ¥150 – ¥350
  • Chimelong Safari Park: ¥280 – ¥380
  • Shamian Island (free, except for museums and cafes)
  • Chen Clan Ancestral Hall: ¥10 – ¥20
  • Pearl River night cruise: ¥100 – ¥300
  • Guangdong Museum (free entry, but reservation required)

4. Transportation Costs for Tourists

  • Metro day pass: ¥20 – ¥30
  • Taxi fare (average trip within the city): ¥25 – ¥80
  • Didi (ride-hailing app): ¥30 – ¥100 per trip

If you plan to stay in Guangzhou for a week, a public transport budget of ¥200 – ¥400 should be sufficient.

Estimated Total Travel Budget

Budget Traveler (Backpacker Style)

  • Accommodation: ¥100 – ¥300 per night
  • Food: ¥50 – ¥150 per day
  • Transport: ¥20 – ¥40 per day
  • Attractions & activities: ¥50 – ¥150 per day
  • Total daily cost: ¥220 – ¥640
  • Total for a 5-day trip: ¥1,100 – ¥3,200

Mid-Range Traveler (Comfortable Stay & Dining Out)

  • Accommodation: ¥400 – ¥800 per night
  • Food: ¥200 – ¥500 per day
  • Transport: ¥40 – ¥80 per day
  • Attractions & activities: ¥150 – ¥400 per day
  • Total daily cost: ¥790 – ¥1,780
  • Total for a 5-day trip: ¥3,950 – ¥8,900

Luxury Traveler (High-End Hotels & Fine Dining)

  • Accommodation: ¥1,200 – ¥3,000 per night
  • Food: ¥800+ per day
  • Transport: ¥100 – ¥300 per day
  • Attractions & activities: ¥300 – ¥800 per day
  • Total daily cost: ¥2,400 – ¥4,900
  • Total for a 5-day trip: ¥12,000 – ¥24,500

Final Thoughts on Costs in Guangzhou

Guangzhou offers great flexibility in terms of daily expenses, making it an accessible destination for travelers with different budgets. Whether you’re a backpacker seeking budget street food or a luxury traveler looking for five-star experiences, the city provides diverse options without breaking the bank.
