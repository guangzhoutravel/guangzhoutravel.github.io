A Romantic Weekend in Guangzhou: Best Spots for Couples

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Guangzhou, the vibrant capital of Guangdong Province, is often overshadowed by its glitzy neighbor, Hong Kong. But for couples seeking a mix of culture, cuisine, and cozy moments, this city is a hidden gem. From strolling along the Pearl River to savoring dim sum at dawn, here’s how to craft the perfect romantic weekend in Guangzhou.

Day 1: Exploring the Heart of the City

Morning: Breakfast at a Traditional Teahouse

Start your day like locals do—with a steaming pot of tea and an array of delicate dim sum. Lian Xiang Lou (莲香楼) is a century-old teahouse where you can share fluffy char siu bao (barbecue pork buns) and silky har gow (shrimp dumplings). The vintage ambiance, with its wooden carvings and red lanterns, sets the tone for a nostalgic morning.

Midday: A Stroll Through Shamian Island

Step back in time on Shamian Island, a leafy enclave of colonial-era architecture. Once a foreign concession, its cobblestone streets and European-style buildings make it feel like a mini Paris. Hold hands as you wander past pastel-hued mansions, then stop for coffee at Starbucks Reserve (yes, even chains feel charming here).

Afternoon: Romance on the Pearl River

Hop on a Pearl River cruise for breathtaking skyline views. Opt for the sunset slot—golden hour casts a magical glow over landmarks like Canton Tower and Guangzhou Opera House. Some cruises offer dinner packages, but we recommend saving your appetite for the next stop.

Evening: Dinner at a Rooftop Restaurant

For a meal with a view, head to The Riverside at the Rosewood Guangzhou. Their fusion Cantonese dishes (think honey-glazed pork with truffle) pair perfectly with the glittering cityscape. Alternatively, Mercato offers Italian fare with a side of romance—think candlelit tables and live jazz.

Day 2: Nature and Nightlife

Morning: Sunrise at Baiyun Mountain

Wake up early for a hike up Baiyun Mountain (Cloud Mountain). The 30-minute climb to the summit rewards you with misty vistas and a quiet moment together. Pro tip: Pack a picnic of egg tarts and milk tea from KFC (trust us, their local breakfast menu hits differently).

Midday: Artistic Vibes in Redtory

Channel your inner bohemian at Redtory Art & Design Zone, a repurposed factory complex turned creative hub. Browse contemporary galleries, snap couple photos in front of graffiti walls, and refuel at The Happy Monk with craft beers and loaded nachos.

Afternoon: Shopping (or Window Shopping) in Tianhe

For luxury lovers, Taikoo Hui in Tianhe District is a must. Even if you’re not splurging on designer bags, the mall’s sleek architecture and people-watching are entertainment enough. Pop into Lady M for matcha mille crêpes—because sharing dessert is mandatory.

Night: Cocktails and Karaoke

End your weekend with a bang at Hope & Sesame, a speakeasy-style bar hidden behind a fake noodle shop door. Sip on lychee martinis before belting out Cantopop duets at Partyworld KTV. Bonus points if you attempt a Jacky Cheung classic!

Hidden Gems for Couples

Yuexiu Park’s Secret Garden

Tucked inside Yuexiu Park, the Zhenhai Tower area has secluded benches and lotus ponds—ideal for stolen kisses away from crowds.

Qifeng Pagoda at Night

Most tourists visit by day, but the Qifeng Pagoda lights up dramatically after dark. The surrounding Liwan District also has quaint alleyways perfect for post-dinner exploring.

DIY Pottery at Panjiu Creative Zone

Get hands-on at a pottery studio like Mao Mao Chong, where you can craft matching mugs (and laugh at each other’s lopsided creations).

Pro Tips for a Smooth Trip

  • Transport: Use the metro (clean and cheap) or DiDi (China’s Uber) to avoid taxi hassles.
  • Language: Learn "ngo5 oi3 nei5" (Cantonese for "I love you") for extra brownie points.
  • Weather: Spring (March–May) and autumn (October–November) offer the most pleasant temps.

Whether you’re foodies, culture buffs, or just in need of quality time, Guangzhou delivers romance with a side of adventure. So pack your bags—and your camera—for a weekend you won’t forget.
