Guangzhou Street Food: What to Eat & Where

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Guangzhou, the bustling capital of Guangdong Province, is a paradise for food lovers. Known as the "Culinary Capital of China," this vibrant city offers an endless array of street food that will tantalize your taste buds. From sizzling skewers to steaming dim sum, Guangzhou’s street food scene is a must-experience for any traveler. Here’s your ultimate guide to the best street food in Guangzhou and where to find it.

The Must-Try Street Foods in Guangzhou

1. Dim Sum (点心, Dianxin)

No trip to Guangzhou is complete without indulging in dim sum. These bite-sized delights are a cornerstone of Cantonese cuisine. Some must-try varieties include:

  • Har Gow (虾饺): Translucent shrimp dumplings with a delicate, chewy wrapper.
  • Siu Mai (烧卖): Open-topped pork and shrimp dumplings, often topped with crab roe.
  • Char Siu Bao (叉烧包): Fluffy steamed buns filled with sweet and savory barbecued pork.

Where to Try It: Head to Yum Cha (饮茶) restaurants like Lian Xiang Lou (莲香楼) or Tao Tao Ju (陶陶居) for an authentic dim sum experience.

2. Wonton Noodles (云吞面, Wonton Mein)

A comforting bowl of wonton noodles is a Guangzhou classic. The dish features thin, springy egg noodles topped with plump shrimp and pork wontons in a clear, flavorful broth.

Where to Try It: Baoji Noodle Shop (宝记面家) in Xiguan is a local favorite.

3. Roast Goose (烧鹅, Siu Ngo)

Crispy-skinned and succulent, roast goose is a Cantonese delicacy. The meat is marinated in a blend of spices, roasted until golden, and served with a side of plum sauce.

Where to Try It: Yue Kee (裕记烧鹅饭店) in Shamian is legendary for its roast goose.

4. Clay Pot Rice (煲仔饭, Bo Zai Fan)

This hearty dish features rice cooked in a clay pot with toppings like preserved meats, mushrooms, and a crispy rice crust. The smoky aroma and textures make it irresistible.

Where to Try It: Wong’s Claypot Rice (黄鳝世家) near Beijing Road is a top pick.

5. Cheung Fun (肠粉)

Silky rice noodle rolls stuffed with shrimp, beef, or vegetables, then drizzled with sweet soy sauce. Some versions are topped with fried dough (炸两, Zha Leung).

Where to Try It: Yin Ji Chang Fen (银记肠粉) is a famous chain with multiple locations.

6. Stinky Tofu (臭豆腐, Chou Doufu)

Love it or hate it, stinky tofu is a bold street food experience. The fermented tofu is deep-fried until crispy and served with chili sauce.

Where to Try It: Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street (上下九步行街) has plenty of vendors selling this pungent snack.

7. Sugarcane Juice (蔗汁, Zhe Zhi)

A refreshing drink made from freshly pressed sugarcane, often served with a hint of lemon or ginger.

Where to Try It: Street stalls in Beijing Road (北京路) frequently sell this sweet thirst-quencher.

Best Streets & Markets for Street Food

Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street (上下九步行街)

This historic shopping street is packed with food stalls and old-school eateries. Don’t miss:
- Pan-Fried Dumplings (煎饺, Jian Jiao)
- Fish Balls (鱼蛋, Yu Dan)
- Egg Tarts (蛋挞, Dan Ta)

Beijing Road (北京路)

A mix of modern and traditional, Beijing Road is great for:
- Grilled Oysters (烤生蚝, Kao Sheng Hao)
- Skewered Meat (串串, Chuan Chuan)

Nansha Wet Market (南沙市场)

For a more local experience, this market offers fresh seafood and street snacks like:
- Steamed Rice Rolls (蒸肠粉, Zheng Chang Fen)
- Fried Crab (炸蟹, Zha Xie)

Tips for Enjoying Guangzhou Street Food

  • Go Early: Some stalls sell out by midday.
  • Follow the Crowds: If locals are lining up, it’s probably good.
  • Bring Cash: Many vendors don’t accept digital payments.
  • Be Adventurous: Try something new—you might discover a new favorite!

Guangzhou’s street food is a feast for the senses, blending tradition, innovation, and bold flavors. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned foodie, the city’s culinary treasures will leave you craving more.
