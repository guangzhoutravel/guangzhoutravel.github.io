How to Make Friends in Guangzhou as a Solo Traveler

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Traveling solo can be one of the most liberating experiences, but it can also feel lonely if you don’t know how to connect with others. Guangzhou, a bustling metropolis in southern China, is a fantastic destination for solo travelers looking to meet new people. With its vibrant food scene, rich history, and welcoming locals, the city offers countless opportunities to forge friendships. Here’s your ultimate guide to making friends in Guangzhou as a solo traveler.

Embrace the Local Food Culture

Guangzhou is famous for its Cantonese cuisine, and food is one of the easiest ways to break the ice.

Visit Dim Sum Restaurants

Dim sum isn’t just a meal—it’s a social experience. Head to popular spots like Lian Xiang Lou or Tao Tao Ju and don’t be shy about sharing a table with strangers. Many locals are happy to chat over a steamer basket of har gow (shrimp dumplings) or siu mai.

Join a Food Tour

Signing up for a food tour is a great way to meet fellow travelers and food enthusiasts. Companies like Lost Plate Guangzhou organize group tours where you can bond over delicious street food in neighborhoods like Beijing Road or Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street.

Night Markets & Street Food Stalls

Places like Zhu Jiang New Town Night Market are perfect for striking up conversations. Ask someone for recommendations—most locals are proud of their food culture and love sharing their favorites.

Stay in Social Accommodations

Where you stay can make a huge difference in your social experience.

Hostels with Common Areas

Hostels like Guangzhou Youth Hostel or Pop Hostel have communal spaces where travelers gather. Join a game night or simply hang out in the lounge—you’ll likely find someone to explore the city with.

Couchsurfing or Homestays

Platforms like Couchsurfing connect travelers with locals who offer free stays. Even if you don’t stay overnight, many hosts are happy to meet for coffee or show you around.

Explore Popular Hangout Spots

Guangzhou has plenty of places where people naturally gather.

Shamian Island

This historic area with colonial architecture is a favorite for photographers and leisurely strolls. Strike up a conversation with someone taking pictures—travelers and locals alike are often open to chatting.

Pearl River Cruises

A sunset cruise on the Pearl River is a relaxing way to meet people. Opt for a group tour or shared boat ride, and you might end up making friends while admiring the skyline.

Baiyun Mountain

Hiking Baiyun Mountain is a popular activity, especially on weekends. Join a hiking group or simply ask fellow hikers for tips—many are happy to have company on the trail.

Join Social Events & Meetups

Guangzhou has a thriving expat and traveler community, with regular events where you can meet like-minded people.

Language Exchange Meetups

Check out Meetup.com or Guangzhou Expats groups for language exchange events. Even if you don’t speak Mandarin, many locals attend to practice English and are eager to chat.

Bar Hopping in Party Areas

Areas like Zhu Jiang New Town and Tianhe District have lively bars where solo travelers can mingle. Try spots like Hooley’s Irish Pub or The Brew, which often host trivia nights or live music.

Cultural Workshops

Sign up for a calligraphy class, tea ceremony, or cooking workshop. These activities attract both locals and foreigners, making them ideal for making connections.

Use Social Apps & Online Communities

Technology can be a solo traveler’s best friend.

WeChat Groups

WeChat is the most popular messaging app in China. Join groups like "Guangzhou Expats" or "Guangzhou Travel Buddies" to find people organizing outings.

Tinder & Bumble

While often used for dating, apps like Tinder and Bumble BFF can help you find friends. Many locals and expats use these platforms to meet new people platonically.

Reddit & Facebook Groups

Subreddits like r/Guangzhou or Facebook groups such as "Guangzhou Expats Network" are great for asking questions and finding events.

Learn a Few Basic Mandarin Phrases

Even if you’re not fluent, making an effort goes a long way.

Useful Phrases:

  • Nǐ hǎo! (Hello!)
  • Nǐ huì shuō Yīngyǔ ma? (Do you speak English?)
  • Wǒ shì lǚxíng zhě. (I’m a traveler.)
  • Zhè ge cài hǎo chī ma? (Is this dish delicious?)

Locals appreciate when foreigners try to speak their language, and it can lead to friendlier interactions.

Be Open & Approachable

Sometimes, making friends is as simple as smiling and saying hello.

Start Conversations in Cafés

Guangzhou has many cozy cafés like Starbucks Reserve or local favorites like C.P.C. Coffee. Sit at the communal table and ask someone about their drink—coffee lovers are usually happy to chat.

Attend Festivals & Public Events

Guangzhou hosts events like the Canton Fair or Spring Festival celebrations. These gatherings are perfect for meeting people in a festive atmosphere.

Volunteer or Join a Sports Group

If you’re staying longer, consider volunteering or joining a local sports team. Organizations like Guangzhou Ultimate Frisbee or Hash House Harriers welcome newcomers.

Making friends as a solo traveler in Guangzhou isn’t just possible—it’s almost guaranteed if you put yourself out there. Whether through food, social events, or simply being open to new experiences, you’ll find that the city’s warmth extends beyond its tropical climate.
