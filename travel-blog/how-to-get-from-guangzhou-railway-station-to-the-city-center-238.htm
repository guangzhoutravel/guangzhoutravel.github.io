How to Get from Guangzhou Railway Station to the City Center

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Guangzhou Railway Station is one of the busiest transport hubs in southern China, handling millions of passengers every year. Located in the Yuexiu District, it is well-connected to various parts of the city, but navigating the transportation options can be overwhelming, especially for first-time visitors. Whether you are heading to Tianhe, Zhujiang New Town, or Haizhu, multiple options are available, including the metro, buses, taxis, and even bike-sharing services. Understanding these options can help you travel efficiently while avoiding common pitfalls such as traffic congestion and overpricing.

Taking the Metro: The Fastest and Most Convenient Option

The Guangzhou Metro system is the most efficient way to reach the city center. It is fast, affordable, and well-connected, making it the best choice for travelers who want to avoid traffic jams.

Metro Line 2: Direct Connection to Key Areas

  • Guangzhou Railway Station is served by Metro Line 2, which runs north to south.
  • If you are heading to Gongyuanqian Station, one of the main central transfer points, it’s only two stops away.
  • To reach Tianhe District, transfer to Metro Line 3 at Gongyuanqian Station and continue to Tiyu Xilu Station.

Travel Time and Cost

  • To Gongyuanqian (Beijing Road area): 2 stops, about 4 minutes, fare: ¥2.
  • To Tianhe (Tiyu Xilu Station): 5 stops, about 10 minutes, fare: ¥4.
  • To Zhujiang New Town: Transfer to Line 3 at Tiyu Xilu Station, total travel time: 15 minutes, fare: ¥5.

Buying Tickets

  • Ticket vending machines are available at the metro station, supporting cash, Alipay, and WeChat Pay.
  • A single journey ticket costs between ¥2-6, depending on distance.
  • Tourists can also use Yang Cheng Tong, a rechargeable metro and bus card.

Taking a Taxi: A Direct but Potentially Expensive Option

Taxis in Guangzhou are widely available, but fares can vary depending on traffic conditions and the time of day.

Estimated Taxi Fares to Popular Areas

  • Beijing Road Pedestrian Street (Yuexiu District): 4 km, ¥20-25, 10-15 minutes.
  • Tianhe District (Tiyu Xilu or Zhujiang New Town): 10 km, ¥40-60, 20-30 minutes.
  • Haizhu District (Canton Tower area): 12 km, ¥50-70, 25-35 minutes.

Things to Keep in Mind

  • Always ask the driver to use the meter to avoid overcharging.
  • Avoid unofficial taxis or "black cabs" (unlicensed vehicles) outside the station, as they often charge inflated rates.
  • Payment methods: Most taxis accept cash, Alipay, and WeChat Pay.

Using Ride-Hailing Apps: A More Reliable Alternative to Taxis

If you prefer a more comfortable experience, using ride-hailing apps like Didi Chuxing is a great option.

Why Choose Didi Over Regular Taxis?

  • Fixed pricing displayed before confirming the ride.
  • No need to negotiate fares.
  • More options, including economy, premium, and larger vehicles for groups.

How to Use Didi Chuxing?

  1. Download the Didi Chuxing app (available in English).
  2. Enter your destination and select your preferred ride type.
  3. Pay via WeChat Pay, Alipay, or linked credit cards.

Estimated Didi fares:

  • To Tianhe District: ¥30-50.
  • To Zhujiang New Town: ¥35-55.

Taking the Bus: A Budget-Friendly but Slower Option

Buses are the cheapest way to travel in Guangzhou, but they can be slower due to frequent stops and potential traffic.

Bus Routes from Guangzhou Railway Station to Key Areas

  • Bus No. 7 → Gongyuanqian (Beijing Road area).
  • Bus No. 30 → Tianhe Sports Center.
  • Bus No. 211 → Canton Tower.

Fare and Payment

  • Most city buses charge ¥2 per ride.
  • Payment methods: Cash (exact fare needed) or Yang Cheng Tong card.

While buses are affordable, they are not ideal for travelers with luggage or those unfamiliar with Guangzhou’s bus network.

Using Shared Bikes: A Scenic Option for Short Distances

For those who enjoy cycling, bike-sharing services like Mobike and Hellobike provide an eco-friendly way to explore Guangzhou’s streets.

Where to Find Bikes?

  • Shared bikes are parked near metro stations and bus stops.
  • Unlock them using Alipay or WeChat Pay.

Recommended Routes for Short Bike Rides

  • Guangzhou Railway Station → Yuexiu Park: 10 minutes.
  • Guangzhou Railway Station → Beijing Road Pedestrian Street: 15-20 minutes.

Common Travel Mistakes to Avoid

1. Falling for Taxi Scams

Some taxi drivers near Guangzhou Railway Station refuse to use meters or overcharge passengers. To avoid this:

  • Insist on using the meter.
  • Use Didi Chuxing for a guaranteed fair price.
  • Walk a short distance from the station to flag down a regular taxi.

2. Traveling During Rush Hour

Guangzhou’s metro and roads can be extremely crowded between 7:30-9:30 AM and 5:00-7:30 PM. If possible, travel outside these hours to avoid delays.

3. Not Preparing for Digital Payments

  • Most public transportation services in Guangzhou accept Alipay, WeChat Pay, or Yang Cheng Tong cards.
  • Cash is accepted, but having a mobile payment option makes transactions easier.

4. Not Knowing Your Destination’s Metro Exit

Many metro stations in Guangzhou have multiple exits. Before arriving at your destination, check which exit is closest to your final stop to avoid unnecessary walking.

Guangzhou offers a variety of transportation options to reach the city center from Guangzhou Railway Station. The metro is the most efficient and affordable choice, while taxis and Didi provide direct routes for those carrying luggage. Buses are cost-effective but slower, and bike-sharing is ideal for short trips. Understanding these options will help ensure a smooth journey as you explore the vibrant city of Guangzhou.
