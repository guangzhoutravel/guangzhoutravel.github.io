2025 Guangzhou Travel: Airline & Baggage Policies

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Guangzhou, the vibrant metropolis of Southern China, continues to be a top destination for travelers in 2025. With its rich cultural heritage, cutting-edge architecture, and world-class cuisine, the city promises an unforgettable experience. However, before you pack your bags, it’s crucial to understand the latest airline and baggage policies to ensure a smooth journey. Here’s everything you need to know for your 2025 trip to Guangzhou.

Airline Options for Guangzhou in 2025

Major Airlines Flying to Guangzhou

Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport (CAN) is one of the busiest hubs in Asia, served by numerous international and domestic carriers. In 2025, travelers can choose from a wide range of airlines, including:

  • China Southern Airlines – The largest carrier operating in Guangzhou, offering extensive domestic and international routes.
  • Cathay Pacific – A premium option with excellent service and frequent flights from Hong Kong.
  • Singapore Airlines – Known for its luxury and efficiency, connecting Guangzhou to Southeast Asia and beyond.
  • Qatar Airways – A top choice for travelers from the Middle East and Europe.
  • Delta Air Lines & United Airlines – Providing direct and connecting flights from the U.S.

New Routes and Expanded Services

2025 brings exciting updates for Guangzhou-bound travelers:

  • Increased Direct Flights from Europe: Lufthansa and Air France have added more weekly flights from Frankfurt and Paris.
  • Low-Cost Carrier Expansion: Airlines like Spring Airlines and Scoot now offer budget-friendly options from neighboring Asian countries.
  • Premium Economy Upgrades: Many carriers, including China Southern, have enhanced their premium economy cabins for long-haul comfort.

Baggage Policies: What’s Changed in 2025?

Checked Luggage Allowances

Baggage policies vary by airline, but here’s a general overview for 2025:

  • Economy Class: Typically allows 1-2 checked bags (23kg/50lbs each) on international flights.
  • Business/First Class: Often permits 2-3 checked bags (32kg/70lbs each).
  • Low-Cost Carriers: Strictly enforce weight limits (often 15-20kg) with hefty fees for excess baggage.

Pro Tip: Always check your airline’s website before flying, as policies can change frequently.

Carry-On Rules

Most airlines follow these guidelines for 2025:

  • 1 Cabin Bag + 1 Personal Item: Standard for full-service airlines (e.g., China Southern, Cathay Pacific).
  • Strict Size Limits: Typically 55cm x 40cm x 20cm (22” x 16” x 8”).
  • Weight Restrictions: Usually 7-10kg (15-22lbs) for cabin luggage.

Budget airlines like AirAsia and Scoot may charge extra for carry-ons, so read the fine print!

Restricted and Prohibited Items

Guangzhou’s airport security remains stringent in 2025. Avoid packing:

  • Liquids Over 100ml (must fit in a transparent, resealable bag).
  • Power Banks Above 160Wh (banned on most flights).
  • Sharp Objects (including scissors and knives).

Special Note: Guangzhou customs may inspect food items, so declare any edible souvenirs.

Smart Packing Tips for Guangzhou

Weather-Appropriate Clothing

Guangzhou’s subtropical climate means hot, humid summers and mild winters. Pack:

  • Lightweight, breathable fabrics for summer.
  • A light jacket for cooler evenings or air-conditioned spaces.
  • Comfortable walking shoes – the city is best explored on foot!

Essential Travel Gadgets

  • Universal Adapter: China uses Type A, C, and I plugs.
  • Portable Wi-Fi or Local SIM Card: Stay connected without roaming fees.
  • Translation App: While many signs are bilingual, a little Mandarin (or Cantonese) goes a long way.

Must-Have Documents

  • Passport with at least 6 months validity.
  • Visa (if required) – Check China’s latest entry policies.
  • Printed copies of hotel reservations and flight details.

Navigating Baiyun International Airport in 2025

Terminal Upgrades

Baiyun Airport’s Terminal 2 expansion is now complete, featuring:

  • Faster immigration lanes with biometric scanning.
  • More duty-free shopping and luxury boutiques.
  • Enhanced lounges with showers and workspaces.

Transportation to the City

  • Metro Line 3: The fastest way to downtown Guangzhou (approx. 30 mins).
  • Taxis & Ride-Sharing: Available 24/7; use Didi (China’s Uber) for convenience.
  • Airport Buses: Affordable but slower during peak hours.

Final Thoughts Before You Fly

Traveling to Guangzhou in 2025 is easier than ever, but staying updated on airline and baggage policies ensures a hassle-free trip. Whether you’re indulging in dim sum, exploring the Canton Tower, or diving into the city’s bustling markets, proper preparation lets you focus on the adventure ahead. Safe travels!
