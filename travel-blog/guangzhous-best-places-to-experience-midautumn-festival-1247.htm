Guangzhou’s Best Places to Experience Mid-Autumn Festival

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

The Mid-Autumn Festival, known as Zhōngqiū Jié (中秋节), is one of the most enchanting times to visit Guangzhou. As the city glows under the golden light of the full moon, locals and visitors alike gather to celebrate with mooncakes, lanterns, and lively cultural events. If you're planning a trip to Guangzhou during this magical festival, here are the best places to immerse yourself in the festivities.

1. Yuexiu Park: Lantern Displays & Moonlit Strolls

A Festival of Lights

Yuexiu Park transforms into a dazzling wonderland during the Mid-Autumn Festival. The park’s iconic Five Rams Statue and artificial lakes become the backdrop for elaborate lantern displays. Traditional Chinese motifs, mythical creatures, and glowing moon-shaped installations create a dreamy atmosphere perfect for evening strolls.

Cultural Performances

Local troupes often perform lion dances, Cantonese opera, and folk music near the park’s central square. Don’t miss the chance to see artisans crafting handmade lanterns—kids (and adults!) can even try making their own.

2. Shamian Island: A Historical Moonlit Escape

Colonial Charm Under the Moon

Shamian Island, with its European-style architecture and tree-lined streets, offers a quieter but equally enchanting Mid-Autumn experience. The soft glow of lanterns reflecting off the Pearl River makes it a romantic spot for couples.

Tea Houses & Mooncake Tasting

Many of Shamian’s historic tea houses serve special Mid-Autumn menus featuring premium teas paired with traditional and modern mooncake flavors. Try the classic lotus seed paste or the trendy durian-filled varieties.

3. Canton Tower: Sky-High Celebrations

Moon Viewing from Above

For an unforgettable perspective, head to the Canton Tower’s observation deck. The city’s skyline, illuminated by thousands of lights, looks even more spectacular under the full moon. Some years, the tower hosts special light shows synchronized with traditional music.

Luxury Mid-Autumn Dinners

Several high-end restaurants near the tower offer festival-themed dinners. Imagine savoring gourmet mooncakes while gazing at the moon from one of Guangzhou’s tallest landmarks.

4. Liwan Lake Park: A Waterfront Festival

Boat Rides & Floating Lanterns

Liwan Lake Park is famous for its nighttime boat tours during the festival. Glide across the water as floating lanterns create a shimmering path—a truly magical sight.

Local Street Food Galore

The park’s surrounding streets burst with food stalls selling festival treats like pomelo (a symbolic fruit), roasted chestnuts, and sticky rice balls. Don’t leave without trying lián róng bāo (lotus seed buns), a local favorite.

5. Beijing Road Pedestrian Street: Shopping & Festive Cheer

Mid-Autumn Market Madness

Beijing Road becomes a hub for festival shopping, with vendors selling everything from hand-painted lanterns to artisanal mooncakes. Look out for limited-edition gift boxes from famous bakeries like Guangzhou Jiuxin.

Interactive Cultural Exhibits

Pop-up exhibitions along the street often showcase the history of the Mid-Autumn Festival, including ancient moon-worshipping rituals and the legend of Cháng'é (the Moon Goddess).

6. Baiyun Mountain: A Nature Lover’s Moon Festival

Hiking Under the Moonlight

For those who prefer a more active celebration, Baiyun Mountain offers night hikes with guided tours leading to the best moon-viewing spots. The crisp autumn air and panoramic views make it a refreshing escape from the city bustle.

Picnics with a View

Pack a basket with mooncakes, tea, and seasonal fruits, and join locals for a moonlit picnic at one of the mountain’s scenic overlooks.

7. Pearl River Night Cruise: A Floating Celebration

Dinner Cruises with Live Music

Several cruise operators host special Mid-Autumn dinner cruises along the Pearl River. Enjoy a buffet of Cantonese delicacies while traditional musicians set the mood.

Fireworks & Light Shows

If you’re lucky, your cruise might coincide with a fireworks display over the river—a breathtaking way to cap off the festival night.

8. Chen Clan Ancestral Hall: Traditional Arts & Crafts

Folk Art Demonstrations

This historic complex often holds workshops on festival-related crafts, such as paper lantern making and calligraphy. It’s a great place to learn about the cultural significance of the holiday.

Mooncake-Making Classes

Some local chefs offer hands-on sessions where you can learn to bake your own mooncakes—perfect for bringing a taste of Guangzhou’s Mid-Autumn Festival back home.

Final Tips for Enjoying Mid-Autumn in Guangzhou

  • Book Early: Hotels and cruises fill up quickly during the festival.
  • Try Regional Mooncakes: Guangzhou is famous for its lǎopó bǐng (wife cakes) and yèróng bǐng (coconut mooncakes).
  • Dress for the Weather: September evenings can be warm but breezy—light layers are ideal.

Whether you’re admiring lanterns in Yuexiu Park, cruising the Pearl River, or tasting mooncakes on Shamian Island, Guangzhou offers endless ways to celebrate the Mid-Autumn Festival like a local.
