Guangzhou’s Best Bubble Tea Spots for Solo Travelers

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Traveling solo in Guangzhou is an adventure filled with vibrant street markets, towering skyscrapers, and, of course, an incredible bubble tea scene. Whether you're exploring the city’s historic districts or modern shopping hubs, a refreshing cup of boba is the perfect companion. Here’s a curated list of the best bubble tea spots in Guangzhou for solo travelers, blending must-visit locations with hidden gems.

The Iconic Chains You Can’t Miss

HeyTea (喜茶)

HeyTea is a household name in China, and Guangzhou is no exception. Known for its premium ingredients and innovative flavors, this spot is ideal for solo travelers looking to indulge in a high-quality boba experience. Try their signature Cheese Tea or seasonal fruit teas, which are always a hit. The minimalist, Instagram-worthy interiors make it a great place to relax and recharge.

Nayuki (奈雪の茶)

Another heavyweight in the bubble tea world, Nayuki combines tea with freshly baked pastries—perfect for a quick solo snack break. Their Strawberry Cheese Tea is a crowd-pleaser, and the spacious seating areas in their Guangzhou outlets provide a cozy spot to people-watch or plan your next stop.

Hidden Gems for the Adventurous Sipper

Songshu Xiaozhu (松鼠小筑)

Tucked away in Tianhe District, this whimsical café offers bubble tea with a twist. Their Taro Milk Tea with Crystal Boba is a must-try, and the forest-themed décor makes it a peaceful escape from the city’s hustle. Solo travelers will appreciate the quiet ambiance and friendly staff.

ChaTraMue (手标泰茶)

For those craving Thai-style bubble tea, ChaTraMue delivers an authentic taste of Bangkok in Guangzhou. The Thai Milk Tea with Pearls is rich and aromatic, and the vibrant red cups add a pop of color to your travel photos. It’s a small shop, but the bold flavors are worth the visit.

Bubble Tea with a View

Luk Yu Tea House (陆羽茶室)

While not a traditional bubble tea spot, this historic teahouse in Shamian Island offers a unique twist on the boba experience. Pair their Jasmine Milk Tea with dim sum for a solo dining experience that blends tradition and modernity. The colonial-era architecture adds a nostalgic charm.

Coco Fresh Tea & Juice (都可)

With locations near Canton Tower and Pearl River, Coco is a reliable choice for solo travelers on the go. Grab their Bubble Milk Tea and enjoy it by the riverfront as you take in Guangzhou’s stunning skyline. The affordability and consistency make it a no-brainer.

Trendy Spots for the Social Solo Traveler

The Alley (鹿角巷)

Famous for its Brown Sugar Deerioca Milk, The Alley is a hotspot for aesthetically pleasing drinks. The Liwan District outlet often attracts a young crowd, making it easy to strike up a conversation with fellow boba enthusiasts.

Tiger Sugar (老虎堂)

Another contender in the brown sugar boba game, Tiger Sugar’s No.1 Black Sugar Boba Milk is a creamy, caramelized delight. The bustling atmosphere in their Beijing Road location adds to the excitement of exploring Guangzhou’s shopping districts.

Final Sips for the Solo Explorer

Guangzhou’s bubble tea scene is as diverse as the city itself. From iconic chains to quirky independents, there’s a cup for every solo traveler’s taste. So grab your drink, hit the streets, and let the flavors of Guangzhou guide your journey.
