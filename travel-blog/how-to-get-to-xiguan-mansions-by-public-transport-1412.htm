How to Get to Xiguan Mansions by Public Transport

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xiguan Mansions, a hidden gem in Guangzhou, is a must-visit for travelers who love history, culture, and stunning architecture. Nestled in the heart of Liwan District, this area is famous for its traditional Cantonese-style mansions, vibrant local life, and delicious street food. But how do you get there using public transport? Whether you're a budget traveler or just prefer the convenience of Guangzhou’s efficient transit system, this guide will help you navigate your way to Xiguan Mansions with ease.

Why Visit Xiguan Mansions?

Before diving into the logistics, let’s talk about why Xiguan Mansions should be on your itinerary.

A Glimpse into Old Guangzhou

Xiguan was once the wealthiest district in Guangzhou, home to merchants and elites during the Qing Dynasty. The mansions here, with their unique blend of Western and Cantonese architectural styles, tell stories of a bygone era. Walking through the narrow alleys (called "longtang") feels like stepping back in time.

Foodie Paradise

No trip to Xiguan is complete without trying its famous snacks. From "shuangpi nai" (double-layer milk pudding) to "lianrong bao" (lotus seed buns), the area is a culinary hotspot. Many of these eateries are family-run businesses that have been around for generations.

Photographer’s Dream

The contrast between the old mansions and modern Guangzhou creates a perfect backdrop for photography. Whether you're an amateur or a pro, you’ll find endless inspiration in the intricate carvings, tiled roofs, and bustling street scenes.

Getting to Xiguan Mansions by Metro

Guangzhou’s metro system is one of the most efficient in China, and it’s the easiest way to reach Xiguan Mansions.

Step 1: Take Line 1 or Line 6

The closest metro station to Xiguan Mansions is Changshou Lu Station (长寿路站) on Line 1. If you’re coming from Tianhe or the city center, this is your best bet. Alternatively, you can take Line 6 and get off at Huangsha Station (黄沙站), which is also within walking distance.

Step 2: Exit and Walk

From Changshou Lu Station:
- Take Exit D and walk south along Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street (上下九步行街).
- After about 10 minutes, you’ll start seeing the traditional Xiguan-style buildings.

From Huangsha Station:
- Take Exit B and head northeast toward Enning Road (恩宁路), one of the most well-preserved streets in Xiguan.
- The walk takes around 15 minutes, but the scenery along the way is worth it.

Taking the Bus to Xiguan Mansions

If you prefer buses, Guangzhou’s extensive network has several routes that stop near Xiguan Mansions.

Popular Bus Routes

  • Bus 2, 3, or 6: These buses stop at Shangxiajiu Lu (上下九路), just a short walk from the mansions.
  • Bus 82 or 86: These routes pass by Enning Road, another great entry point.

Tips for Bus Travel

  • Use apps like Gaode Maps (Amap) or Baidu Maps to check real-time bus schedules.
  • Have your Yang Cheng Tong (Guangzhou’s public transport card) ready for seamless boarding.

Exploring Xiguan Mansions on Foot

Once you arrive, the best way to experience Xiguan is by walking. Here are some key spots to check out:

Enning Road (恩宁路)

This historic street is lined with restored mansions, boutique cafes, and traditional workshops. Don’t miss:
- Yongqing Fang (永庆坊): A revitalized cultural hub with art galleries and craft stores.
- Liwan Museum (荔湾博物馆): A great place to learn about Xiguan’s history.

Baohua Road (宝华路)

Famous for its food stalls, this is where you’ll find:
- Chen Tianji Herbal Tea (陈添记鱼皮): A century-old shop serving refreshing herbal drinks.
- Shunji Desserts (顺记冰室): Try their legendary mango pudding.

Final Tips for Your Visit

  • Best Time to Go: Early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds.
  • Cash is King: Some small vendors don’t accept mobile payments.
  • Wear Comfortable Shoes: The cobblestone streets can be uneven.

Now that you know how to get there, all that’s left is to enjoy the charm of Xiguan Mansions!
