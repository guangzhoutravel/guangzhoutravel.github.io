Best Day Trips from Guangzhou – Extend Your Itinerary

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Guangzhou, the bustling capital of Guangdong Province, is a gateway to some of southern China’s most captivating destinations. While the city itself offers endless attractions—from the Canton Tower to Shamian Island—there’s a whole world of adventure just a short trip away. Whether you’re craving ancient villages, scenic mountains, or coastal escapes, these day trips will enrich your travel experience.

1. Foshan: The Birthplace of Kung Fu and Ceramics

Why Visit Foshan?

Just 30 minutes by train from Guangzhou, Foshan is a cultural gem known for its martial arts heritage, traditional ceramics, and vibrant Cantonese opera scene.

Top Attractions

  • Foshan Ancestral Temple (Zumiao): This 600-year-old temple honors the martial arts legend Wong Fei-hung and features stunning Lingnan architecture.
  • Nanfeng Kiln: One of the oldest still-operating kilns in the world, where you can try your hand at pottery-making.
  • Liang Garden: A classical Chinese garden showcasing intricate wood carvings and tranquil ponds.

How to Get There

Take the Guangzhou-Foshan Metro Line (Guangfo Line) or a high-speed train from Guangzhou South Station (around 20 minutes).

2. Zhaoqing: A Scenic Lakeside Retreat

Why Visit Zhaoqing?

Known as the "Guilin of Guangdong," Zhaoqing offers breathtaking karst landscapes, serene lakes, and lush greenery—perfect for nature lovers.

Top Attractions

  • Seven Star Crags (Qixingyan): A stunning park with limestone peaks, caves, and a picturesque lake. Rent a bike or take a boat ride for the best views.
  • Dinghu Mountain: A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve with hiking trails, waterfalls, and ancient temples.
  • Zhaoqing Ancient City Wall: A well-preserved Ming Dynasty relic offering panoramic city views.

How to Get There

High-speed trains from Guangzhou South Station take about 1 hour.

3. Shenzhen: Futuristic City & Theme Park Thrills

Why Visit Shenzhen?

From cutting-edge tech hubs to world-class amusement parks, Shenzhen is a must-visit for modern explorers.

Top Attractions

  • Window of the World: A theme park featuring miniature replicas of global landmarks like the Eiffel Tower and Taj Mahal.
  • Dafen Oil Painting Village: A quirky art district where you can buy or commission affordable paintings.
  • OCT Loft Creative Culture Park: A hipster haven with galleries, cafes, and street art.

How to Get There

High-speed trains from Guangzhou East Station take 30-40 minutes.

4. Kaiping: UNESCO-Listed Diaolou Towers

Why Visit Kaiping?

This lesser-known gem is home to the iconic diaolou—fortified towers blending Chinese and Western architectural styles.

Top Attractions

  • Zili Village: The most famous cluster of diaolou, offering a glimpse into 19th-century overseas Chinese heritage.
  • Chikan Ancient Town: A riverside town with colonial-era buildings and nostalgic charm.
  • Li Garden: A lavish mansion complex with gardens, bridges, and European-inspired designs.

How to Get There

Buses from Guangzhou’s provincial bus station take about 2 hours.

5. Huizhou: Coastal Beauty & Hot Springs

Why Visit Huizhou?

For a mix of beach relaxation and cultural exploration, Huizhou delivers with its golden shores and historic Hakka villages.

Top Attractions

  • Xunliao Bay: A pristine beach with clear waters, perfect for swimming and seafood feasts.
  • West Lake (Huizhou): A scenic lake surrounded by pagodas and walking paths.
  • Nankun Mountain Hot Springs: Soak in therapeutic mineral waters amid lush forests.

How to Get There

High-speed trains from Guangzhou East Station take around 1.5 hours.

6. Qingyuan: Adventure & Nature Escapes

Why Visit Qingyuan?

Dubbed the "Drift Capital of China," Qingyuan is ideal for adrenaline junkies and outdoor enthusiasts.

Top Attractions

  • Gulongxia Glass Bridge: A thrilling suspension bridge with a transparent walkway high above a canyon.
  • Yingxi Fenglin Corridor: A scenic area with waterfalls, bamboo forests, and hiking trails.
  • Hot Springs Resorts: Unwind in one of the many natural hot spring resorts.

How to Get There

Buses or trains from Guangzhou take about 1.5 hours.

7. Zhuhai: Seaside Charm & Island Hopping

Why Visit Zhuhai?

With its coastal promenades, luxury resorts, and proximity to Macau, Zhuhai is a fantastic getaway.

Top Attractions

  • Lover’s Road: A romantic seaside path with stunning ocean views.
  • Chimelong Ocean Kingdom: One of the world’s largest aquariums, featuring whale sharks and roller coasters.
  • Wailingding Island: A tranquil island escape with white sandy beaches.

How to Get There

High-speed ferries from Guangzhou’s Nansha Port take around 2 hours.

8. Conghua: Hot Springs & Countryside Serenity

Why Visit Conghua?

Just an hour from Guangzhou, this rural retreat is famous for its hot springs and lychee orchards.

Top Attractions

  • Conghua Hot Springs: Luxurious resorts with private mineral baths.
  • Liuxi River National Forest Park: A peaceful spot for hiking and bamboo rafting.
  • Lychee Picking (Seasonal): Sample fresh lychees straight from the trees in summer.

How to Get There

Metro Line 14 or buses from Guangzhou take about 1 hour.

9. Shaoguan: Danxia Landform & Ancient Temples

Why Visit Shaoguan?

Home to the otherworldly Danxia Mountain, this destination is a paradise for photographers and hikers.

Top Attractions

  • Danxia Mountain: A UNESCO-listed geological wonder with rainbow-colored rock formations.
  • Nanhua Temple: A sacred Buddhist site dating back over 1,500 years.
  • Maba Man Relics: Explore prehistoric caves with ancient human fossils.

How to Get There

High-speed trains from Guangzhou South Station take about 1 hour.

10. Yangshuo (Guilin): A Longer but Worthwhile Escape

Why Visit Yangshuo?

Though slightly farther (4-5 hours), Yangshuo’s dreamy karst landscapes and Li River cruises are unforgettable.

Top Attractions

  • Li River Cruise: Drift past iconic limestone peaks.
  • West Street (Yangshuo): A lively pedestrian street with cafes and souvenir shops.
  • Bike Riding in the Countryside: Explore rice paddies and rural villages on two wheels.

How to Get There

High-speed trains from Guangzhou South Station take about 2.5 hours to Guilin, followed by a 1-hour transfer to Yangshuo.

Each of these day trips offers a unique way to experience southern China beyond Guangzhou’s urban energy. Whether you're into history, nature, or modern thrills, there’s an adventure waiting just a short journey away.
