5 Days in Guangzhou: A Themed Cantonese Opera Tour

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Guangzhou, the vibrant capital of Guangdong Province, is a city where modernity and tradition collide in the most fascinating ways. While its towering skyscrapers and bustling markets often steal the spotlight, the city’s rich cultural heritage—particularly its deep connection to Cantonese opera—is a treasure waiting to be explored. This 5-day themed tour is designed for travelers who want to immerse themselves in the art, history, and culinary delights of Guangzhou, all while celebrating the grandeur of Cantonese opera.

Day 1: Arrival and the Heart of Cantonese Opera

Morning: Check-In at a Themed Hotel

Start your journey by staying at a hotel that pays homage to Cantonese opera. The Liyuan Hotel near Yuexiu District features décor inspired by traditional opera costumes and masks. The lobby even has a small stage where performances are held on weekends.

Afternoon: Explore the Cantonese Opera Art Museum

Head to the Cantonese Opera Art Museum in Liwan District, a stunning architectural masterpiece that blends Lingnan-style gardens with exhibits on opera history. Here, you’ll find vintage costumes, scripts, and even interactive displays where you can try on digital opera makeup.

Evening: A Night at the Redtory Opera House

Wrap up your first day with a live performance at Redtory Opera House, a converted factory space that now hosts contemporary interpretations of classic operas. The mix of traditional melodies and modern staging is a perfect introduction to the art form.

Day 2: The History Behind the Art

Morning: Visit the Chen Clan Ancestral Hall

This UNESCO-listed site isn’t just a ancestral hall—it’s a living museum of Cantonese craftsmanship. The intricate wood carvings and ceramic figurines often depict scenes from famous operas. Don’t miss the opera-themed guided tour offered every Tuesday and Friday.

Afternoon: Dim Sum and Opera Trivia

Stop by Lian Xiang Lou, a century-old teahouse where dim sum is served alongside snippets of opera history. Try the char siu bao (barbecue pork buns) while listening to stories about legendary performers like Ma Si Tsang.

Evening: Backstage Tour at Guangdong Opera House

The Guangdong Opera House, designed by Zaha Hadid, is a futuristic marvel. Arrange for a backstage tour to see how modern technology is used to preserve traditional opera. If you’re lucky, you might catch a rehearsal!

Day 3: Hands-On Opera Experience

Morning: Makeup and Costume Workshop

At the Guangzhou Opera Troupe Training Center, you can participate in a workshop where professionals teach you how to apply the elaborate jing (painted face) makeup. You’ll also get to wear a replica costume and strike a few dramatic poses.

Afternoon: Learn the Moves

Join a beginner’s class on opera gestures and fan techniques. The exaggerated movements and delicate hand flourishes are harder than they look!

Evening: Street Food and Shadow Puppetry

Stroll through Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street, where vendors sell lo mai gai (sticky rice chicken) and other Cantonese snacks. End the night at a small theater showcasing shadow puppetry, a folk art closely tied to opera traditions.

Day 4: Day Trip to Foshan – The Birthplace of Cantonese Opera

Morning: Journey to Foshan

Just an hour from Guangzhou, Foshan is where Cantonese opera took root. Visit the Foshan Ancestral Temple, which has a dedicated opera stage dating back to the Qing Dynasty.

Afternoon: Martial Arts and Opera Fusion

Foshan is also the hometown of Wing Chun, a martial art that influenced opera’s fight choreography. Take a short class at a local school to see the connection firsthand.

Evening: Return to Guangzhou for a Banquet

Back in the city, indulge in a Cantonese opera-themed banquet at Panxi Restaurant. Dishes are named after famous operas, like The Purple Hairpin (a delicate seafood soup).

Day 5: Modern Takes and Souvenir Hunting

Morning: Contemporary Opera Exhibition

Visit the Guangdong Museum of Art for an exhibition on how young artists are reimagining opera through digital media and installations.

Afternoon: Shop for Opera Memorabilia

Browse the stalls at Qingping Market for souvenirs like miniature opera masks or vintage vinyl records of classic performances.

Evening: Farewell Performance

End your trip with a rooftop opera performance at The Ritz-Carlton Guangzhou. Sip on a lychee martini as the city’s skyline provides a breathtaking backdrop to the final act of your journey.

This 5-day tour is more than just sightseeing—it’s a deep dive into the soul of Cantonese culture. Whether you’re a longtime opera fan or a curious traveler, Guangzhou’s blend of history, art, and flavor will leave you enchanted.
