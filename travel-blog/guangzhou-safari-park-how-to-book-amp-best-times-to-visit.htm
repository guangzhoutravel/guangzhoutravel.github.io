Guangzhou Safari Park: How to Book & Best Times to Visit

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Guangzhou Safari Park is one of China’s most impressive wildlife attractions, offering an unforgettable experience for animal lovers, families, and adventure seekers. Located in Panyu District, this massive park is home to over 20,000 animals, including rare species like giant pandas, white tigers, and African elephants. Whether you're planning a family outing or a solo adventure, knowing how to book tickets and choosing the best time to visit can make your trip smoother and more enjoyable.

Why Visit Guangzhou Safari Park?

A World-Class Wildlife Experience

Unlike traditional zoos, Guangzhou Safari Park provides a more immersive experience, allowing visitors to observe animals in habitats closely resembling their natural environments. The park is divided into two main sections: the Drive-Through Safari Zone and the Walking Safari Zone.

  • Drive-Through Safari Zone: Visitors can explore this area by car or the park’s shuttle bus, getting up close to free-roaming lions, giraffes, and zebras.
  • Walking Safari Zone: This section features themed exhibits, animal shows, and interactive experiences like feeding giraffes or watching the famous Panda Village.

Unique Animal Encounters

One of the park’s biggest draws is its Giant Panda Center, where visitors can see these adorable creatures munching on bamboo or playing in their enclosures. Other highlights include:
- White Tiger Exhibit – A rare chance to see these majestic predators.
- Koala Sanctuary – One of the few places in China where you can observe koalas.
- Elephant Performance – A fun and educational show featuring these gentle giants.

How to Book Tickets for Guangzhou Safari Park

Online Booking (Recommended)

The easiest way to secure your tickets is through official platforms like:
- Ctrip (Trip.com)
- Meituan
- Klook
- The park’s official WeChat mini-program

Steps to book online:
1. Visit the preferred booking platform.
2. Select the date and ticket type (adult, child, or senior).
3. Choose optional add-ons (e.g., safari bus tour, animal feeding experiences).
4. Complete payment and receive an e-ticket via email or app.

On-Site Ticket Purchase

If you prefer buying tickets in person, head to the park’s main entrance. However, be prepared for long queues, especially on weekends and holidays.

Pro Tip: Weekdays are less crowded, so if you’re flexible, avoid weekends and public holidays.

Ticket Prices (2024)

  • Adult Ticket: ¥300-350 (approx. $40-50)
  • Child Ticket (1.2m-1.5m): ¥200-250 (approx. $30-35)
  • Senior Ticket (65+): Discounts available (check official site)
  • VIP Experiences: Additional fees for behind-the-scenes tours or private animal encounters.

Best Times to Visit Guangzhou Safari Park

Ideal Seasons

The park is open year-round, but certain seasons offer better weather and animal activity:

Spring (March-May)

  • Pleasant temperatures (20-28°C / 68-82°F).
  • Animals are more active after winter.
  • Blooming flowers enhance the park’s scenery.

Autumn (September-November)

  • Cool, comfortable weather.
  • Fewer crowds compared to summer.
  • Ideal for outdoor exploration.

Worst Times to Visit

Summer (June-August)

  • Extremely hot and humid (up to 35°C / 95°F).
  • Higher chance of rain.
  • Peak tourist season means longer wait times.

Winter (December-February)

  • Some animals may be less active.
  • Chilly mornings and evenings.

Best Time of Day

  • Morning (9 AM - 12 PM): Animals are most active during feeding times.
  • Late Afternoon (3 PM - 5 PM): Fewer crowds, great for photography.
  • Avoid Midday (12 PM - 2 PM): Hottest part of the day; many animals rest.

Must-See Attractions & Shows

1. Safari on Wheels (Drive-Through Experience)

  • Duration: ~45 minutes.
  • Best for: Families with kids.
  • Highlights: Lions, tigers, bears, and rhinos roaming freely.

2. Giant Panda Center

  • Feeding Times: 10 AM & 2 PM (best viewing).
  • Don’t Miss: The adorable red pandas nearby!

3. Animal Performances

  • Elephant Show: 11 AM & 3 PM.
  • Bird Paradise Flight Show: 12 PM & 4 PM.

4. Walking Safari Zones

  • African Savannah: Giraffes, zebras, and ostriches.
  • Amazon Rainforest: Exotic birds and reptiles.

Tips for a Smooth Visit

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes (the park is huge!).
  • Sunscreen and a hat (especially in summer).
  • A refillable water bottle (hydration stations available).
  • Camera or smartphone for wildlife photography.

Park Etiquette

  • Do not feed animals unless in designated areas.
  • No flash photography (it stresses the animals).
  • Follow safety instructions in the Drive-Through zone.

Getting There

By Metro:
- Take Line 3 to Hanxi Changlong Station, then a short taxi ride.

By Car/Taxi:
- Parking available (¥20-30 for the day).

Pro Tip: Arrive early (before 10 AM) to avoid crowds and secure better animal sightings.

Special Events & Seasonal Highlights

Lunar New Year (January/February)

  • Themed decorations and cultural performances.
  • Special animal enrichment activities.

Halloween (October)

  • Nighttime safari tours (spooky but fun!).
  • Themed shows and decorations.

Christmas (December)

  • Festive lights and holiday-themed animal encounters.

Final Thoughts

Guangzhou Safari Park is a must-visit for anyone traveling to southern China. With proper planning—booking tickets in advance, choosing the right season, and arriving early—you’ll maximize your experience and create unforgettable memories. Whether you're marveling at giant pandas or embarking on a safari adventure, this park offers something for every wildlife enthusiast.
