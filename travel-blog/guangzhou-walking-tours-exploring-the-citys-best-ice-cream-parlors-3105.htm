Guangzhou Walking Tours: Exploring the City’s Best Ice Cream Parlors

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Guangzhou, a bustling metropolis in southern China, is famous for its rich history, vibrant culture, and—most importantly—its incredible food scene. While dim sum and Cantonese cuisine often steal the spotlight, the city’s ice cream parlors are a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Whether you're a local or a traveler with a sweet tooth, a walking tour of Guangzhou’s best ice cream spots is a delicious way to explore the city.

Why Ice Cream in Guangzhou?

Guangzhou’s tropical climate makes it the perfect place to enjoy frozen treats year-round. But beyond the weather, the city’s ice cream scene is a fascinating blend of traditional flavors, modern twists, and international influences. From artisanal gelato to innovative bubble tea-infused soft serve, Guangzhou’s parlors offer something for every palate.

The Ultimate Ice Cream Walking Tour Route

1. Start at Tianhe District: The Trendy Hub

Tianhe is Guangzhou’s modern heart, filled with shopping malls, cafes, and—of course—some of the city’s best ice cream spots.

Sweet Monster (太古汇店)

Located in Taikoo Hui, Sweet Monster is famous for its playful, Instagram-worthy soft serve topped with colorful cereal, cotton candy, and fresh fruit. Their signature "Monster Balls" (tapioca pearls) add a fun texture to every scoop.

Venchi (天环广场)

For those who prefer Italian gelato, Venchi in Tee Mall is a must-visit. Their rich, creamy flavors—like dark chocolate with hazelnut or mango sorbet—are made with premium ingredients. Don’t skip their signature chocolate-dipped cones!

2. Wander Through Yuexiu: History Meets Dessert

Yuexiu District is home to some of Guangzhou’s most iconic landmarks, including Yuexiu Park and the Canton Tower. After sightseeing, cool off with these ice cream gems.

Mabelle (北京路店)

A local favorite, Mabelle serves up delicate French-inspired desserts, including their famous rose lychee ice cream. The floral sweetness paired with lychee’s refreshing taste is pure bliss on a hot day.

GODIVA (广州塔店)

Located near the Canton Tower, this luxury chocolate brand’s ice cream is worth the splurge. Their double chocolate soft serve, drizzled with melted GODIVA chocolate, is a decadent treat.

3. Explore Liwan: Old-School Charm & Hidden Gems

Liwan District, with its historic streets and traditional shophouses, offers a nostalgic backdrop for discovering unique ice cream flavors.

Luneurs (永庆坊店)

This artisanal parlor in Yongqing Fang specializes in French-style ice cream with a local twist. Try their salted caramel or the seasonal osmanthus-flavored scoop—a nod to Guangzhou’s love for floral notes.

Milk Cow (荔枝湾店)

A small-batchain known for organic milk-based treats, Milk Cow’s honeycomb ice cream is a standout. The creamy milk flavor pairs perfectly with crunchy honeycomb chunks.

4. End in Haizhu: Riverside Cool-Down

Haizhu District, with its scenic Pearl River views, is the perfect place to wrap up your ice cream adventure.

Emack & Bolio’s (琶醍店)

This American import in Party Pier offers wild, over-the-top cones stacked with cereal, marshmallows, and sprinkles. Their "Space Cake" flavor (vanilla with chocolate cake pieces) is a crowd-pleaser.

DQ (珠江新城店)

Sometimes, you just crave a classic. Dairy Queen’s Blizzards—especially the Oreo or matcha versions—are a reliable way to end your tour on a high note.

Pro Tips for Your Ice Cream Crawl

  • Go early – Some parlors, like Sweet Monster, attract long lines, especially on weekends.
  • Share portions – Ice cream melts fast in Guangzhou’s heat, so smaller servings let you try more flavors.
  • Ask for seasonal specials – Many shops rotate flavors based on local fruits like lychee or longan.
  • Pair with tea – Some traditional parlors serve ice cream with oolong or jasmine tea to balance the sweetness.

Whether you're a dessert enthusiast or just looking for a fun way to explore Guangzhou, this ice cream walking tour offers a tasty journey through the city’s diverse neighborhoods. So grab a spoon, lace up your walking shoes, and get ready to discover Guangzhou one scoop at a time!
