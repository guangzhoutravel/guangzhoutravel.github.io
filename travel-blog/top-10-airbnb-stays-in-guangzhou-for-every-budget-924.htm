Top 10 Airbnb Stays in Guangzhou for Every Budget

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Guangzhou, the bustling metropolis of southern China, is a city where tradition meets modernity. From its towering skyscrapers to its historic temples, there’s something for every traveler. Whether you're a budget backpacker or a luxury seeker, Airbnb offers a wide range of accommodations to suit your needs. Here are the top 10 Airbnb stays in Guangzhou for every budget, each offering a unique experience.

Luxury Stays: Splurge-Worthy Retreats

1. Sky-High Penthouse with Pearl River Views

Location: Zhujiang New Town
Budget: $$$$

For those who want to experience Guangzhou in style, this penthouse in Zhujiang New Town is a dream come true. Floor-to-ceiling windows offer panoramic views of the Pearl River and Canton Tower. The sleek, modern design includes a private terrace, a gourmet kitchen, and a jacuzzi. Perfect for couples or business travelers who want to indulge.

2. Historic Mansion Turned Boutique Loft

Location: Shamian Island
Budget: $$$$

Shamian Island is known for its colonial-era architecture, and this meticulously restored mansion is a gem. High ceilings, antique furniture, and a tranquil courtyard create an old-world charm. The loft includes a private chef service upon request, making it ideal for travelers who appreciate history and exclusivity.

3. Ultra-Modern Villa with Private Pool

Location: Baiyun District
Budget: $$$$

Escape the city’s hustle in this secluded villa nestled in Baiyun’s lush greenery. The property features a private infinity pool, a home theater, and a rooftop garden. It’s perfect for families or groups looking for a lavish retreat without sacrificing privacy.

Mid-Range Gems: Comfort Without Breaking the Bank

4. Chic Apartment Near Canton Tower

Location: Haizhu District
Budget: $$-$$$

This stylish apartment is just a short walk from Canton Tower, making it a prime spot for sightseers. The interior blends Scandinavian minimalism with local art, and the balcony offers stunning skyline views. Great for solo travelers or couples who want a central location.

5. Cozy Artist’s Loft in Tianhe

Location: Tianhe District
Budget: $$

A creative haven in the heart of Tianhe, this loft is filled with original artwork and vintage decor. The host, a local artist, often leaves personalized recommendations for hidden cafes and galleries. Ideal for culture enthusiasts who want an authentic Guangzhou experience.

6. Riverside Studio with Bike Rentals

Location: Liwan District
Budget: $$

Overlooking the Pearl River, this studio comes with complimentary bike rentals, allowing guests to explore the scenic riverbanks. The space is small but efficiently designed, with a kitchenette and a cozy reading nook. A fantastic pick for active travelers.

Budget-Friendly Finds: Affordable Yet Memorable

7. Minimalist Pod Hostel Near Beijing Road

Location: Yuexiu District
Budget: $

For backpackers and solo adventurers, this pod-style hostel offers privacy at a fraction of hotel prices. Each pod has charging ports, reading lights, and blackout curtains. The communal lounge is a great place to meet fellow travelers, and Beijing Road’s famous street food is just steps away.

8. Traditional Cantonese Courtyard House

Location: Xiguan, Liwan
Budget: $

Immerse yourself in local culture with a stay in this restored Xiguan courtyard house. The wooden beams and tiled roofs reflect classic Lingnan architecture. Though basic, the stay includes a homemade dim sum breakfast—an unbeatable value for budget-conscious travelers.

9. High-Rise Shared Flat with City Views

Location: Panyu District
Budget: $

This shared flat in Panyu offers a clean, modern space with a stunning city view from the 30th floor. The host provides free metro passes for short-term guests, making it easy to explore Guangzhou’s attractions without spending much on transport.

10. Quirky Capsule Hotel Near Chimelong Paradise

Location: Panyu District
Budget: $

If you’re visiting Chimelong Resort, this futuristic capsule hotel is a fun and affordable option. Each capsule has mood lighting, USB ports, and soundproof walls. The shared lounge features arcade games and a mini-library, ensuring entertainment after a day at the theme park.

Final Tips for Booking Your Guangzhou Airbnb

  • Peak Seasons: Avoid Chinese New Year and Canton Fair dates if you want lower prices.
  • Transport: Opt for stays near metro stations (e.g., Line 3 or 5) for easy access.
  • Local Eats: Ask hosts for recommendations—some hidden food spots aren’t on tourist maps!

No matter your budget, Guangzhou’s Airbnb scene has something to make your trip unforgettable. Happy travels!
