Cheap Day Trips from Guangzhou

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Guangzhou, a bustling metropolis in southern China, is not only a hub for business and culture but also a fantastic base for exploring nearby destinations. Whether you're a local looking for a quick escape or a traveler with limited time, there are plenty of affordable day trips to enjoy. From ancient villages to scenic mountains, here are some of the best budget-friendly excursions you can take from Guangzhou.

1. Foshan: A Blend of Tradition and Modernity

Just a 30-minute train ride from Guangzhou, Foshan is a city steeped in history and culture. Known for its martial arts heritage (it’s the birthplace of Bruce Lee’s Wing Chun master, Ip Man), Foshan offers a mix of traditional Cantonese charm and modern attractions.

Top Attractions in Foshan

  • Ancestral Temple (Zumiao): This 1,000-year-old temple is dedicated to the Northern God and features intricate carvings and traditional architecture.
  • Lingnan Tiandi: A revitalized district with old-style shophouses, trendy cafes, and craft stores.
  • Foshan Nanfeng Ancient Kiln: A historic ceramic kiln that’s still in operation, offering a glimpse into the city’s porcelain-making legacy.

Cost-Saving Tip: The metro connects Guangzhou and Foshan, making transportation cheap and convenient. Entry fees to most attractions are under ¥50.

2. Qingyuan: Nature and Hot Springs

For those craving nature, Qingyuan is a perfect getaway. Located about an hour from Guangzhou by bus, this area is famous for its lush landscapes and relaxing hot springs.

Must-Visit Spots in Qingyuan

  • Nanshan Mountain: A great spot for hiking with panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Gudou Hot Springs: A budget-friendly hot spring resort where you can soak in mineral-rich waters for under ¥100.
  • Feilai Gorge: A scenic area with waterfalls and glass-bottomed walkways for thrill-seekers.

Budget Hack: Skip the private tours and take a public bus from Guangzhou’s provincial bus station for under ¥40 round-trip.

3. Zhaoqing: Karst Landscapes and Lakes

Zhaoqing, a two-hour bus ride from Guangzhou, is often called the "Guilin of Guangdong" for its stunning karst mountains and serene lakes.

Highlights of Zhaoqing

  • Seven Star Crags (Qixingyan): A picturesque park with limestone peaks and tranquil lakes—perfect for a leisurely boat ride.
  • Dinghu Mountain: A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve with hiking trails and ancient temples.
  • Zhaoqing Ancient City Wall: A well-preserved section of Ming Dynasty history.

Affordable Eats: Try the local zongzi (sticky rice dumplings) from street vendors for a cheap and delicious snack.

4. Conghua: Hot Springs and Countryside Vibes

Less than an hour from Guangzhou, Conghua is a rural retreat known for its hot springs and lychee orchards.

What to Do in Conghua

  • Wenquan Hot Springs: A budget-friendly alternative to luxury resorts, with public pools starting at ¥60.
  • Liuxi River National Forest Park: Ideal for kayaking, bamboo rafting, or just enjoying the greenery.
  • Lychee Picking (Seasonal): If visiting in summer, don’t miss the chance to pick fresh lychees straight from the trees.

Transport Tip: Shared vans from Guangzhou’s Tianhe District cost around ¥25 per person.

5. Huizhou: Beaches and Historic Towns

If you’re willing to travel a bit farther (about two hours by bus), Huizhou offers coastal scenery and charming old towns.

Best Places to Explore in Huizhou

  • Xunliao Bay: A quiet beach with clear waters, perfect for a relaxing day by the sea.
  • Huizhou Ancient City: Wander through narrow alleys lined with traditional Hakka architecture.
  • West Lake (Huizhou): A scenic lake surrounded by pagodas and gardens.

Money-Saving Advice: Pack a picnic to avoid overpriced seafood restaurants near tourist spots.

6. Kaiping: UNESCO-Listed Diaolou Towers

A bit farther out (around two and a half hours by bus), Kaiping is home to the unique diaolou—fortified towers built by overseas Chinese in the early 20th century.

Why Visit Kaiping

  • Diaolou Villages (Zili, Liyuan, etc.): These UNESCO-listed towers blend Chinese and Western architectural styles.
  • Movie Connections: Some scenes from Let the Bullets Fly were filmed here.
  • Rural Scenery: Cycling through the countryside is a great way to explore.

Budget Tip: Entry to most diaolou clusters is under ¥80, and group tours from Guangzhou can be as low as ¥200.

7. Shenzhen (Yes, It Can Be Cheap!)

While Shenzhen is known for its high-tech vibe and shopping, there are ways to enjoy it without breaking the bank.

Low-Cost Shenzhen Activities

  • Dafen Oil Painting Village: Browse (or even commission) affordable art in this quirky district.
  • Shenzhen Bay Park: A free waterfront park with great skyline views.
  • OCT Loft Creative Culture Park: A hipster-friendly area with free art exhibitions.

Getting There: High-speed trains from Guangzhou start at ¥80, and metro travel within Shenzhen is inexpensive.

Final Thoughts

With so many affordable day trips from Guangzhou, there’s no excuse to stay cooped up in the city. Whether you’re into history, nature, or just a change of scenery, these destinations offer something for every budget. Pack light, plan ahead, and enjoy exploring Guangdong’s hidden gems!
