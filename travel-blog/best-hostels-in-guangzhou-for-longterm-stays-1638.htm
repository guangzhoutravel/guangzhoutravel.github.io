Best Hostels in Guangzhou for Long-Term Stays

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Guangzhou, the bustling metropolis of Southern China, is a dream destination for digital nomads, budget travelers, and expats looking for an affordable yet vibrant place to stay long-term. With its rich history, mouthwatering Cantonese cuisine, and thriving expat community, the city offers plenty of budget-friendly hostels that cater to extended stays. Whether you're working remotely, studying Mandarin, or simply exploring the Pearl River Delta, these hostels provide comfort, community, and convenience.

Why Choose a Hostel for Long-Term Stays in Guangzhou?

Hostels aren’t just for backpackers passing through—many offer private rooms, coworking spaces, and monthly discounts that make them ideal for long-term residents. Here’s why staying in a Guangzhou hostel could be your best bet:

  • Affordability – Compared to serviced apartments or hotels, hostels offer significantly lower rates, especially with long-term discounts.
  • Social Atmosphere – Meet fellow travelers, expats, and locals in communal areas, events, and coworking spaces.
  • Prime Locations – Many hostels are in central districts like Yuexiu, Tianhe, or Haizhu, close to metro lines and attractions.
  • Flexibility – No rigid leases; you can extend or leave without hefty penalties.

Now, let’s dive into the best hostels in Guangzhou for long-term stays.

Top 5 Hostels in Guangzhou for Extended Trips

1. Lazy Gaga Hostel (Haizhu District)

Best for: Digital nomads & creatives

Located in the artsy Haizhu District, Lazy Gaga is a favorite among remote workers and long-term travelers. The hostel features a rooftop garden, a cozy café, and a dedicated coworking space with high-speed Wi-Fi.

Why Stay Long-Term?
- Monthly discounts (up to 30% off for stays over 4 weeks).
- Private pods for those who need quiet workspaces.
- Weekly events like language exchanges and movie nights.

Nearby Attractions:
- Chimelong Paradise (15-minute metro ride)
- Canton Tower (10 minutes by taxi)

2. Guangzhou Happy Dragon Hostel (Yuexiu District)

Best for: History lovers & budget travelers

Nestled in the heart of Yuexiu, this hostel is steps away from historic sites like the Chen Clan Ancestral Hall and Beijing Road Pedestrian Street. The vibe is laid-back, with a mix of dorm beds and private rooms.

Why Stay Long-Term?
- Cheapest monthly rate in central Guangzhou.
- Free walking tours organized by staff.
- Kitchen access for those who love cooking.

Nearby Attractions:
- Yuexiu Park (5-minute walk)
- Shamian Island (15-minute bike ride)

3. The Loft Hostel (Tianhe District)

Best for: Nightlife & shopping enthusiasts

If you love being near the action, The Loft is just a short walk from Zhengjia Plaza and Taikoo Hui, two of Guangzhou’s top shopping hubs. The hostel has a modern industrial design with a rooftop bar.

Why Stay Long-Term?
- Discounted monthly private rooms (with ensuite bathrooms).
- Free pub crawls every Friday.
- 24/7 reception for late check-ins.

Nearby Attractions:
- Canton Fair Complex (10-minute metro ride)
- Party Pier (5-minute taxi ride)

4. Nomad Capsule Hostel (Panyu District)

Best for: Students & budget-conscious travelers

Panyu is quieter but well-connected via metro, making it a great choice for students attending nearby universities. Nomad Capsule offers futuristic pod-style beds with privacy curtains and personal charging ports.

Why Stay Long-Term?
- Ultra-affordable weekly rates.
- Study lounge with free printing.
- Bike rentals for exploring Panyu’s parks.

Nearby Attractions:
- Chimelong Safari Park (20-minute bus ride)
- Guangzhou Univeristy Town (15-minute bike ride)

5. Zen Rooms Hostel (Baiyun District)

Best for: Nature lovers & yogis

If you prefer a peaceful retreat, Zen Rooms is tucked near Baiyun Mountain, offering meditation sessions and yoga classes. The décor is minimalist with wooden accents and plenty of greenery.

Why Stay Long-Term?
- Wellness programs (free for monthly guests).
- Eco-friendly policies (solar-powered showers).
- Hiking group meetups every weekend.

Nearby Attractions:
- Baiyun Mountain (direct trail access)
- South China Botanical Garden (10-minute taxi ride)

Tips for Booking Long-Term Hostel Stays in Guangzhou

  1. Negotiate Rates – Many hostels offer unpublished discounts for stays over a month. Always ask!
  2. Check Wi-Fi Speed – If you’re working remotely, test the connection before committing.
  3. Look for Kitchen Access – Eating out every day adds up; having a kitchen saves money.
  4. Read Reviews – Platforms like Hostelworld and Booking.com have honest feedback from long-term guests.
  5. Visit First – If possible, book a night or two before committing to a long stay.

Guangzhou’s hostels provide more than just a bed—they offer a community, convenience, and a cost-effective way to experience this dynamic city. Whether you’re here for work, study, or adventure, these hostels ensure your extended stay is comfortable and memorable.
